On Tuesday, a developer named [Sid](https://www.0xsid.com/blog/antigravity-bait-n-switch) discovered that [Antigravity](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/google-io-2026-developer-highlights/), the AI IDE he had been writing production code inside, made by Google, no longer contained an IDE. Google had pushed a background update that silently replaced the editor with a chat window, wiped his configurations, and split the install across two directories so the launcher broke on its own. Terminal gone. Sidebar gone. Settings gone. Recovery instructions, per Google's [own developer forum](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/antigravity-2-0-forced-update-is-a-drag-back-to-the-stone-age-give-us-the-ide-back/146029): roll back to version 1.23.2 by hand and disable auto-updates.

The [forum thread title](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/antigravity-2-0-forced-update-is-a-drag-back-to-the-stone-age-give-us-the-ide-back/146029), filed by a paying customer:

> "Antigravity 2.0 forced update is a drag back to the stone age, give us the IDE back"

[Killed by Google](https://killedbygoogle.com/) lists 290 discontinued Google products. [Reader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Reader) died in 2013. [Stadia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Stadia) died in 2023 after Google spent four years asking game studios to build for it. Sid signed up anyway.

At I/O 2026, the same week, Google [announced Antigravity AI Ultra](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) for $100 a month: five times the AI limits in the product it spent the week removing the editor from. Sid recovered his settings from his old [Cursor](https://cursor.com/) folder, where they had worked fine before he moved to Google.
