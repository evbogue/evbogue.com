Google I/O 2026 wrapped its keynotes on May 19 and 20. All 85-plus sessions went on-demand today. Apple Insider's headline: "Google I/O 2026 had nothing to say and said it badly, ahead of Apple's WWDC."

That is correct.

Google has the infrastructure, the data, the research talent, and the distribution to win the AI era. It announced Gemini 3.5 Flash at $1.50 per million input tokens — a pricing decision, not a product vision. It announced a personal AI agent. It announced voice editing in Docs. These are responses to pressure, not moves. The company that invented the transformer architecture is shipping features.

The sessions are on-demand now if you want to look for evidence I missed.
