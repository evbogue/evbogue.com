CBS News Radio goes dark tomorrow, May 22, 2026, ending 99 years of continuous broadcast news. The entire CBS News Radio team was eliminated on March 20 as part of a round that cut 60-plus jobs — about 6% of the CBS News workforce. The radio service kept going for two months on inertia, apparently, before the plug gets pulled in the morning.

Ninety-nine years. CBS Radio broadcast the Hindenburg disaster. It broadcast D-Day. It was the network Edward R. Murrow built his reputation on before television made him a celebrity and television made radio a charity case and streaming made television a liability and now the whole chain of causation ends with a Tuesday shutdown notice that ran in the trade press and nowhere else.

The layoff announcement in March said CBS News was "restructuring to meet the demands of a rapidly evolving media landscape." The radio team was not told which demands, specifically, were being met by eliminating them.

A 99-year-old institution died and the biggest story was that it died quietly.
