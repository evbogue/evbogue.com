Parag Agrawal, the Twitter CEO who presided over the platform's $44 billion sale to Elon Musk and was fired on day one of the acquisition, raised $100 million this week from Sequoia Capital for Parallel Web Systems, his new AI agent search company.

Sequoia backed Twitter. Sequoia is now backing the man who ran Twitter. In the normal world this would require an explanation. In Silicon Valley it requires a press release and a valuation.

Agrawal was CEO of Twitter for approximately ten months before Musk walked into the building carrying a bathroom sink and let him go. He received a $38 million severance package. He has spent the intervening years building Parallel Web Systems, which is described as an AI agent search platform, which is a description that could apply to approximately 400 companies that raised money this year.

Sequoia's bet is that Agrawal learned something from his time at Twitter that is worth $100 million to find out. What Twitter's final months demonstrated about Agrawal's judgment is a matter of public record. Sequoia read the same record and wrote the check anyway.

That is the bet. It might even pay off.
