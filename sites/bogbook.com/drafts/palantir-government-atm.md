Palantir reported Q1 revenue of $1.63 billion on May 4, with U.S. government revenue up 84% year over year. CEO Alex Karp raised full-year 2026 guidance to 71% growth. The stock popped. The press covered it as a tech earnings story.

It is not a tech earnings story. It is a political access story.

Karp donated $1 million to MAGA Inc. in December 2024 and another $1 million to Trump's inauguration committee. Since then Palantir has received a $30 million ImmigrationOS contract to help prioritize deportations, a $300 million Agriculture Department food-security contract, and a $75 million no-bid contract to monitor federal workers' return-to-office compliance. The U.S. government revenue that grew 84% is, in substantial part, the return on that $2 million political investment.

Karp called the quarter "a reflection of the world's recognition that Palantir has figured out how to deploy AI in the most demanding environments."

The most demanding environment Palantir operates in, it turns out, is Washington. And they've figured that out too.
