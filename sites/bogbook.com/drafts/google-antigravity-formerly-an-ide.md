Google's answer to Cursor was an AI IDE called [Antigravity](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/). This week, Google updated it into a chat box. The chat box genre is already well represented! Google's contribution is that this one used to be your editor.

The update arrived as a background process, which is Google's preferred mechanism for doing things to your computer without asking. It replaced the IDE with a conversational prompt window, wiped chat histories and configurations, and quietly split the install across two separate directories so the launcher broke on its own. Sid, a developer who [wrote about it](https://www.0xsid.com/blog/antigravity-bait-n-switch), clicked his usual shortcut and found his entire development environment gone. Terminal gone. Sidebar gone. Settings gone. The thread on Google's [own developer forum](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/antigravity-2-0-forced-update-is-a-drag-back-to-the-stone-age-give-us-the-ide-back/146029) is titled "Antigravity 2.0 forced update is a drag back to the stone age." Google's customers named it. The recovery instructions: roll back to version 1.23.2 manually and disable auto-updates so the product stops shipping itself at you.

At I/O 2026, Google [announced an AI Ultra plan](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) for $100 a month, promising 5x higher AI limits in Antigravity. Five times the limits. In the chat box where your editor used to be.
