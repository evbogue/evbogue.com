Google shipped a background update to [Antigravity](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) this week that replaced the IDE with a conversational prompt box, wiped chat histories and configurations, and broke the app launcher by quietly splitting the install into two separate directories. Sid, a developer who [wrote about it](https://www.0xsid.com/blog/antigravity-bait-n-switch), found his entire development environment replaced when he clicked his usual shortcut. He was not asked. His settings were gone. The terminal was gone. The sidebar was gone. To recover the IDE, Google's [own developer forum](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/antigravity-2-0-forced-update-is-a-drag-back-to-the-stone-age-give-us-the-ide-back/146029) recommends manually rolling back to version 1.23.2 and disabling auto-updates so the tool stops improving itself at you.

Also at I/O 2026, Google [announced an AI Ultra plan](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) at $100 a month offering 5x higher AI limits in Antigravity. Five times the limits. In the product that just ate your editor.
