Google pushed a background update to [Antigravity](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) on Tuesday that replaced the code editor with a chat window, wiped developer configurations, and broke its own app launcher by silently splitting the install across two directories. Sid, a developer who [documented the damage](https://www.0xsid.com/blog/antigravity-bait-n-switch), opened his usual shortcut and found his terminal, sidebar, and settings gone. Recovery instructions, per Google's [own developer forum](https://discuss.ai.google.dev/t/antigravity-2-0-forced-update-is-a-drag-back-to-the-stone-age-give-us-the-ide-back/146029): manually roll back to version 1.23.2 and disable auto-updates.

The forum thread title, written by a customer, not a critic:

> "Antigravity 2.0 forced update is a drag back to the stone age, give us the IDE back"

[Killed by Google](https://killedbygoogle.com/) tracks 290 discontinued Google products. Google Reader died in 2013. Google Stadia died in 2023 after years of asking game studios to build for it. Antigravity is not on the list yet. The IDE is just no longer an IDE.

At I/O 2026, the same week, Google [announced Antigravity AI Ultra](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/google-launches-antigravity-2-0-with-an-updated-desktop-app-and-cli-tool-at-io-2026/) for $100 a month: five times the AI limits in the product it spent the week removing the editor from.
