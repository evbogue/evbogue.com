SpaceX filed its S-1 with the SEC yesterday, targeting a $1.75 trillion valuation, a $75 billion raise, and a June 12 listing on Nasdaq under ticker SPCX — the largest IPO in history if it prices as planned.

The S-1 shows SpaceX lost $4.94 billion in 2025, a sharp reversal from a $791 million profit the year before. The loss came from SpaceX's all-stock merger with xAI — Musk's AI company, which burns approximately $14 billion in cash per year. xAI, home to the Grok model and the X social media platform, is now a wholly owned SpaceX subsidiary. It is not profitable.

The one profitable segment is Starlink: $11.4 billion in revenue, $4.4 billion in operating income, 63% EBITDA margins. Starlink is a good business. Rockets and AI are not.

Musk will control 85.1% of voting power after the IPO through super-voting Class B shares. The float is 5%. Major U.S. public pension funds have formally opposed the governance structure as "extreme." They will probably buy it anyway.

SpaceX was profitable until Musk merged his money-losing AI company into it. Now it loses $5 billion a year. The roadshow starts June 4.
