NPR offered voluntary buyouts this week to approximately 300 employees, primarily in newsgathering roles, citing an $8 million budget deficit. The deficit exists because the federal government eliminated public media subsidies. The journalists being bought out are, in significant part, the ones who covered the federal government.

The circularity is not subtle. Defund the reporters who cover you; watch the reporters disappear. It is a clean mechanism. It does not require anyone to make a threatening phone call.

NPR's statement called the buyouts a response to "significant financial headwinds." The headwinds have a source. The source is Congress and the administration that instructed it. NPR's statement did not name them.

This is what the end of public media looks like in America in 2026: not a dramatic shutdown, not a fight, just a voluntary separation package and a smaller newsroom that covers Washington with fewer people than it had before Washington cut its budget.

The reporters who remain will cover it straight. That is what they do.
