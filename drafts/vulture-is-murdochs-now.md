I was a photo editor at New York Magazine when we launched The Vulture. Not an important person. I cropped photos, matched images to stories, went to the wrong meetings. But I was in the building when the entertainment blog went live, and there is the small, dumb pride of having been present when something got made.

[James Murdoch bought New York Magazine today](https://www.cnn.com/2026/05/20/media/james-murdoch-buys-new-york-magazine-and-vox-media-podcasts). His holding company Lupa Systems [reportedly paid more than $300 million](https://variety.com/2026/digital/news/james-murdoch-buys-new-york-magazine-vox-media-podcast-network-deal-1236754854/) for New York Magazine, Vox.com, and the Vox podcast network. Vox Media CEO Jim Bankoff is coming along to run what was purchased. The Verge, Eater, and SB Nation were not included. Just the names, the brands, the history, and The Vulture — the blog that used to have some teeth.

The timing had the specific grotesqueness that only media can manage. New York won the National Magazine Award for general excellence this morning, the top prize from the American Society of Magazine Editors. By afternoon the sale was announced. The vulture landed on the award ceremony.

That is not a metaphor I am reaching for. That is just what happened.

Murdoch is picking at the last scraps of a publication that peaked a long time ago. His family name is already on Fox News and the Wall Street Journal. Whether James runs things differently from Rupert is a question I will leave to people who still track that distinction with any hope attached to it. What I know is that a name built over decades just got traded for a check, and the press release quoted him expressing deep commitment to ambitious journalism, which is exactly what a press release says.

New York Magazine was genuinely good for a long time. The Vulture did cultural criticism that mattered. That is the thing being transacted: a reputation, turned into a line item, sold to a dynasty in the business of buying media and remaking it in a more convenient shape.

I left NYMag. I left all of it. I run a small blog now with a subscriber list stored in a JSON file. I have no standing to mourn what happened to a place I worked at briefly, in a minor capacity, fifteen years ago. I was the guy who cropped the photos. The Vulture going to Murdoch is not my loss in any real sense.

But I was in the building when it launched. That is exactly enough to feel this specific kind of dumb about it today.
