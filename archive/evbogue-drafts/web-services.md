<p class="date">October 10, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>It’s tempting for me to believe that I can get everything on the Internet for free. </p>
<p>While it is true that most of the Things I get on the Internet are free, there are a few things that I’ve chosen to pay for because they give me added benefits that I could never get for free.</p>
<p>Obviously this is a continuous balance. If a free service came out tomorrow that was better than one of these paid services, I’d certainly switch over. However, until that is the case, I gladly part with some of my money in order to enjoy certain advantages.</p>
<p><strong>1. <a href="http://eepurl.com/fs1tY">Mailchimp</a></strong>. [Rewards link] I love Mailchimp because it helps me gather permission from readers. It’s one of the first web Apps that I start up during any workday. It gives me sensory perception of what my readers are doing in a way that I haven’t experienced. It handles all of my email needs, including hosting my premium Letter.</p>
<p>I pay $50 a month for Mailchimp, because of how many people are on my lists. This is flexible, Mailchimp has a free version when you’re getting started.</p>
<p><strong>2. <a href="http://www.typekit.com/">Typekit</a></strong>. Do you see the fancy fonts on my site? They’re provided by Typekit. I like that Typekit gives me the ability to embed a wide range of Fonts for only a small price per year. It’s difficult to get Typefaces to display across platform using other ways — this is why I trust Typekit to do that for me.</p>
<p>I pay $49.99 a year for Typekit.</p>
<p><strong>3. <a href="http://www.wufoo.com/">Wufoo</a></strong>. I’m just getting started with the potential of Wufoo — which includes, of course, being able to collect money when I’m signing people up using a form.  I’ve been using it to collect questions that come up in the minds of my readers, and discover how topics I write about land for people.</p>
<p>I pay $29.95 a month to Wufoo. There’s a free version with limited capabilities, if you’re getting started.</p>
