<p>Why did I write <em>Minimalist Business</em>?</p>
<p>To answer a specific question question that my audience kept returning to: how was I able to start a zero overhead online business that runs itself?</p>
<p>A bit of history: in July of 2009, I quit my day job as a photo editor at New York Magazine and jumped on a plane to Portland Oregon with all of my stuff in a bag. By February, I&#8217;d created a Minimalist Business that supported my lifestyle anywhere in the world.</p>
<p>Fast-forward to today, I&#8217;m still grateful for the work that I put in last year, building the foundation that allows me to travel or relocate whenever I want.</p>
<p>I recently finished a three-city tour of the US, uprooting from a retreat in Boulder, CO. I visited New York for a little over two weeks, gathering valuable information for my next project, dropped by Chicago, before heading west. I&#8217;m currently a few days into setting up shop in a city that&#8217;s almost completely new to me: Seattle, WA.</p>
<p>How am I able to <a href="/posts/anywhere">live and work from anywhere</a>?</p>
<p>1. I reduced padding in my life for complete mobility.</p>
<p>2. I reduced business overhead to near zero.</p>
<p>3. I used existing infrastructure to automate distribution.</p>
<p>Obviously, there&#8217;s a lot more to it. That&#8217;s why the book is 125 pages + a 65 page additional PDF added at the end of 2010. The upgraded edition has a 30 page quick start guide &#8212; with step-by-step instructions for those just getting started online.</p>
<p>Preview the <a href="http://issuu.com/evbogue/docs/minimalistbusinesspreviewcopy">first 37 pages here</a>.</p>
<p><a href="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/minimalistbusiness1.jpeg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-310" title="minimalistbusiness1" src="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/05/minimalistbusiness1.jpeg" alt="" width="250" height="250"/></a>Creating a minimalist business has allowed me to:</p>
<p>1. Reduce meaningless work.</p>
<p>I currently work less than 2 hours a week on admin and communications. This allows me to focus on the work that challenges me: experiencing the world and telling my experiences on The Letter.</p>
<p>2. Focus on improving myself.</p>
<p>I studied for my 200 hour yoga certification at the end of last year, and I&#8217;ve been able to invest hundreds of hours improving my skills as a writer.</p>
<p>3. Enjoy my life in the world.</p>
<p>For three years I sat in a cubicle, waiting for my life to start. It didn&#8217;t, until I left. Since, I&#8217;ve lived in 5 different American cities, and learned more about myself and my world than I ever had previously. I couldn&#8217;t have done this without the skills I learned during the first year, and wrote down with a raw fury into Minimalist Business early last year.</p>
<p>To see how I&#8217;m exploring my world, follow me on <a href="http://www.instagr.am/">Instagr.am</a> (@evbogue).</p>
<p>I hope the work can help you do the same.</p>
