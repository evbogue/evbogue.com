<p itemprop="description"><p><a href="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/L1000009-2.jpg"><img src="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/09/L1000009-2.jpg" alt="" title="L1000009-2" width="100%" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1035"/></a></a>For the last year, I decided that every time I needed to drive a car, I’d rent a Zipcar.</p>
<p>I went into my <a href="http://www.mint.com/">Mint</a> today, and discovered that I’d spent $1,450 on Zipcars between September of 2010 – September of 2011. This includes two 4-day trips between cities for adventures.</p>
<p>I was reminded recently by Gwen Bell that the <a href="http://www.gwenbell.com/blog/life-experience-how-ive-saved-nearly-70000-by-not-driving.html">average person spends $9,000 a year</a> owning a car. $1,450 doesn’t seem like much to me, considering I’ve driven many very nice cars (my favorite is the <a href="http://www.minicooperconvertible.net/wp-content/uploads/2011/03/Mini-Cooper-Convertible.jpg">Mini Cooper Convertible</a>). </p>
<p>When I look at Zipcar, I see a company that is revolutionizing the way we live. It’s <strong>taking the burden of ownership away from the individual</strong>. For me, this is amazing. I don’t have to worry about paying for insurance or gas. If the car is damaged while I’m not using it? I don’t have to pay for that damage. Zipcar even cleans and maintains the cars for me.</p>
<p>Zipcar tells me that if I’m in an accident (this hasn’t happened yet), I only have to pay $750. If I was more accident prone, I could offset this price by opting to pay a monthly fee to Zipcar for more personal insurance. </p>
<p>Zipcar also gives me more options: I’ve driven <a href="/posts/shared-ownership">so many different cars</a> in the last year, I’ve never had time to be bored with them. </p>
<p>When I look at all of these facts, I see the ultimate savings in time. I’m might have spent $1,450 on Zipcars last year, but I saved untold amounts in time. Since I’m using Zipcar, I don’t need to invest time in maintaining a car, parking it, making sure that ‘it’s alright’ in the world. Instead, I can relax when I’m not driving a Zipcar. </p>
