<p itemprop="description"><p>I’ve been noticing two types of writers lately.</p>
<p>The first writer has theories about everything. They’re making up their own philosophy about how the world works, and then telling everyone about it.</p>
<p>The second writer has conducted experiments and can tell from experience.</p>
<p>Obviously, with the second, theories came first –they just didn’t tell anyone about them until after they experimented.</p>
<p>Recently, I’ve been asking myself: am I reading someone who’s spinning theories or am I reading someone who’s telling from experience? </p>
<p>Then I ask myself: which kind of work lands best for me? Which type of writing feels more authentic and grounded in reality? Which one of these writers would I pay to read? </p>
<p>And finally I ask myself the hardest question: which one of these writers do I want to be?</p>
