<p itemprop="description"><p>What did you do and how did that go?</p>
<p>The above prompt is the magic formula for landing a piece that gives valuable information to a reader. This prompt cultivates experience telling about two specific elements:</p>
<p>1. Experiments.<br/>
2. Measurements.</p>
<p>I’ve found that my posts land better with my audience when I write in this way.</p>
<p>For example:</p>
<p><strong>What did you do?</strong> For the last two months, I’ve been writing every single day to my blog.</p>
<p><strong>How did that go?</strong> An average of 1,000 people are viewing my blog posts every single day now. This is up from an average 500 people viewing my blog posts every single day, when I was on digital sabbatical during the month of July. So, in essence, two months of writing every day has doubled the number of people who read my blog every day.</p>
<p>Now, my readers can take that information, and devise their own experiments. Will you get similar results if you start writing every day? Only a measured experiment can give you the answer. Hopefully my experiment will give you a feeling of whether or not this is a good option for you.</p>
<p>There’s a lot of room for growth within this formula. I can be as generous as I want to be in giving away information. I can be as simple or as complex as I want.</p>
