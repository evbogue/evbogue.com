<p itemprop="description"><p>It’s my experience that owning Things, in many cases, gives me less options, and restricted freedoms. </p>
<p>This is why I choose to share as much as possible on Things that would otherwise be large expenses.</p>
<p>I’ve never owned a car. Instead, I use <a href="http://www.zipcar.com/apply?promo_code=olyqbooo">Zipcar</a> [rewards link] when I need additional mobility. This saves me from having a car payment, an insurance payment, and buying $4 gasoline (gas is included! which blows my mind.) The cost of ownership becomes disbursed across Zipcar’s network, leading to savings for me. I don’t have to interact with a human to get my car, which leads to less friction — but I know if something goes wrong, there’s humans a phone call away. </p>
<p>Using Zipcar gives me more options (which is a form of freedom.) I also save a lot of money, in comparison to an alternate reality where I owned a car.</p>
<p>On a sunny day, I’ll rent a convertible Mini Cooper. </p>
<p>If I’m taking a long trip, and I need to sleep somewhere, I’ll zip myself a Kia Soul, and throw my futon in the back end. </p>
<p>If I need a cheap get-around car, I’ll zip myself a Honda Insight. </p>
<p>I learned by zipping around in a Prius that I really don’t like driving a Prius. </p>
<p>Last year, when I moved from Oakland to San Francisco, I zipped a flatbed pickup and threw everything I owned in the back of it — including a queen size bed. Having a queen sized bed gave me less options, so I untethered from that.</p>
<p>My experience is that we’re moving towards more tools that give us shared ownership of Things. Zipcar is one example of this. </p>
<p><a href="http://www.spotify.com/">Spotify</a> is another example. I wrote about <a href="/posts/untether-from-music">how I untethered from my music collection</a>. Instead of investing thousands of dollars in records, not to mention the time in ‘keeping up’ with what music is cool, so I don’t buy crap records, I use Spotify to lease my music.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.airbnb.com/">Airbnb</a> is a similar concept to Zipcar, but with living situations. This model of shared housing is just getting started, but I suspect in the next few years, Airbnb will revolutionize the way we live. </p>
<p>I’ve used Airbnb twice. When I landed in Oakland, and when I landed in Seattle. I’m anticipating the day when I’ll be able to drop into any city and grab a spot using Airbnb for a month or two, with everything I need to get settled in within a day, at near-cost to renting an apartment. For now, it’s not quite ideal, but it will be as more adopt the service (or a similar one.)</p>
<p>A great talk on this is Sam Lessin’s <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9XOf-GUMCXk">The End of Ownership</a> at TEDx.</p>
