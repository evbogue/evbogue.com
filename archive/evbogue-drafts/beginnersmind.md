<p><i>Written by Ev Bogue | Follow me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a></i></p>
<p><a href="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/L1000164.jpg"><img class="alignright" src="http://evbogue.com/wp-content/uploads/2011/08/L1000164.jpg" alt="" width="50%"/></a>A few days ago I was sitting in the window of Cafe Vita, watching as pools of falling rain collected in the table outside.</p>
<p>I felt disconnected, because I was. The Internet had been off for nearly a month. I’d <a href="/posts/twitter">untethered from Twitter</a>, a service that I’d come to rely on that had (imo) begun to unravel. In February, I <a href="/posts/clean-slate">blank-slated my blog</a>. Here I was, sitting in a coffee shop, so completely alone — untethered completely in the isolation of a <a href="/posts/digital-sabbatical">digital sabbatical</a>.</p>
<p><em>Have you ever had a moment when you thought you’d figured out everything, only to see that moment slip away?</em> Well, July made me realize that the moment had slipped away. It’s impossible to figure things out in a world that’s constantly changing.</p>
<p>I have no idea what works anymore.</p>
<p>I’m a beginner. As I stumble through the new code on my blog, it feels like the first time.</p>
<p>As I scroll with two fingers on the new Mac OS through the foreign and yet familiar windows of <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a>, I’m starting to see that the next evolution of the Internet is here — and everything I created before (that wasn’t timeless) is quickly slipping into obsolesce.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>The opposite of untethering is gripping.</p>
<p>Gripping is the feeling when a car nearly hits us, and our entire body tenses up in a moment of reflexiveness.</p>
<p>It doesn’t matter that we had no control over whether the car killed us or not. It’s tons of steel, we’re just human — but we grip all the same.</p>
<p>As something starts to slip away, we grip it. We hold on tight in an attempt to make everything stay the same.</p>
<p>But the reality is that nothing stays the same, ever. The universe is constantly changing, and we’re just a small part of that whole. We’re orbiting each other for a brief second in the history of everything. In that brief second, we’re gripping ourselves to everything that we never want to let go of, but will eventually have to.</p>
