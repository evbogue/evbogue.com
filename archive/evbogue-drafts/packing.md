<p><em>February 11th 2011, DEN &#8211;&gt; LAX</em></p>
<p><a href="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5260/5436911430_84b3f09c85_m.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5260/5436911430_84b3f09c85_m.jpg" alt="" width="240" height="240"/></a>I&#8217;m sipping a chai waiting for my flight to board, and it occurred to me just how light my luggage has become. It&#8217;s fascinating to me to step outside myself and look at the belongings that I now have.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m headed back to San Francisco in a few days, where I&#8217;ll be emptying the last bits of stuff from the room I have. Then I&#8217;m on the road again. I&#8217;ll be returning to Boulder a few times before leaving the country, but &#8216;home&#8217; in the traditional sense has evolved into an easy place to find anywhere: where the cloud looks through my iPhone.</p>
<p>A year ago when I declared myself a minimalist, I was carrying a house on my back, compared to my upgraded augmented humanity setup that I travel with now.</p>
<p>I recall barely being able to walk towards the airport in Brooklyn, after I parted paths with New York Magazine. I had something like 97 items in a bag, including an industrial grade bike lock. Silly past me!</p>
<p>Every time I pack up and leave a city, I leave a whole bunch of things behind.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s what I&#8217;m traveling with today:</p>
<p>Backpacking bag, about half-full.<br/>
Laptop bag.<br/>
A pair of hiking shoes<br/>
11&#8243; Macbook Air<br/>
3G Kindle<br/>
iPhone 3GS<br/>
Hoodie<br/>
Jacket<br/>
Long sleeved shirt<br/>
Two pairs of climbing pants<br/>
Three pairs of yoga shorts<br/>
10 pairs of socks<br/>
10 pairs of underwear<br/>
10 tanks of various colors<br/>
Travel towel<br/>
Toothbrush/floss/razor/deodorant<br/>
Moleskin<br/>
Paper-clamp for cards/money<br/>
Two pens and a sharpie<br/>
Compass totem (so I know which direction reality is)<br/>
Key necklace<br/>
Trungpa&#8217;s The Myth of Freedom</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not counting, but you can if you want.</p>
<p><a href="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4100/5436913528_e68f1f7f12_m.jpg"><img alt="" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4100/5436913528_e68f1f7f12_m.jpg" class="alignright" width="240" height="240"/></a>It&#8217;s important to note that I&#8217;m not really living this way because of some sort of extremist life-choice. Instead, I&#8217;ve simply embraced my own personal edge in augmented humanity. Living this way leads to some uncertain situations &#8212; for example, no sleeping bag means I&#8217;m not sure if I&#8217;ll be sleeping in a bed or not some nights.</p>
<p>Living this way for extended periods of time would not be possible without the extended mental senses that come with embracing augmented humanity &#8212; iPhone to Twitter to access the collective + apps that peer from the cloud such as all of Google&#8217;s infrastructure.</p>
