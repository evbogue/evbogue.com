<p><i>Written by Ev Bogue | Follow me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a></i></p>
<p>Last year my old blog, <a href="/posts/farbeyondthestars">Far Beyond The Stars</a>, topped out at 80,000 visitors a month. Now I’m at a slowly rising 18,534 readers. This smaller number is understandable, as I took about 6 months off from blogging regularly as I was (and am) still working with the deeper layer of the premium letter, <a href="http://www.letterly.net/evbogue">Evolving Your Digital Work</a>.</p>
<p>Now, I’m growing the blog again. This time, I want to do it slowly.</p>
<p>It’s easy to grow a blog too quickly.</p>
<p>Throw in a cleaver headline, or trigger the hell out of people, and I’m liable to have a viral post on my hands. Two years ago, I got very good at writing just such posts. Last holiday season I wrote a post called “How to Save $1,300 and be Loved by Everyone Forever”, which went viral, was retweeted multi-hundreds of times, and seen by tens of thousands. It was about consuming mindfully during the holidays.</p>
<p>While in practice, more seems better. In actuality, more isn’t necessarily.</p>
<p>The thing about a viral post, especially one with a trick headline is many of the people coming didn’t necessarily want to read my blog. They just came because there was a crowd, so what must have been happening had to have been important.</p>
<p>My plan this time around is to grow the blog, the premium Letter, and my next e-book mindfully.</p>
<p>I’m not going to be writing viral headlines, sucking up to super-popular bloggers, or selling my soul for eyeballs this time around.</p>
<p>This means I need your help.</p>
<p>If you read a post, and like it. I’d appreciate it if…</p>
<p>…at the very least, tap the +1 button. It helps with Google search rankings, social proof, and other magic. It’s very easy to do, at no cost to you.</p>
<p>…share it! On the primary network you maintain your connections on (in this day in age, that could be many places or one.)</p>
<p>…link to it from your blog. This is the best, but understandably, also the most difficult. I tend to check my trackbacks, and enjoy reading your blog posts.</p>
<p>Thanks for your help.</p>
<p>Best,<br/>
Ev</p>
