<p><i>Written by Ev Bogue | Follow me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a></i></p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>When I <a href="/posts/twitter">untethered from Twitter</a>, I had to delete my account. I couldn’t just leave it open. I would have wondered: did anyone tweet me? Then I would have worried: but what if they tweeted me, and then got mad at me for not replying?</p>
<p>Removing the possibility of being tweeted, my mind was left no space to worry or wonder. </p>
<p>Instead, I started to look for new ways to reach out to new people.</p>
<p>Yes, leaving in this way is absolute. All of my tweets are gone. The 6,000 people who supposedly were following me? Still out there, but the link between us: broken.</p>
<p>In this untethering, I made way for new possibilities. I didn’t starve to death. A sabertooth tiger didn’t eat me. </p>
<p>I just didn’t have the option of being tweeted anymore.</p>
<p>As technology advances, the systems that I use to explore my digital world will change. Over the last decade, I’ve watched dozens of key systems come and go. Livejournal, Myspace, Geocities, Facebook –to name a few that I was an active member, but quit when they stopped being useful for me. </p>
<p>I choose to care my digital self in this way: I clean up when I leave. This isn’t for the Internet, it isn’t for my readers. It’s for me: so I don’t have to worry that someone is desperately trying to get ahold of me on a system I no longer use.</p>
<p>So what system am I using to explore my digital world now? Google+. It’s what all the cool kids are using these days (until the next evolution.)</p>
