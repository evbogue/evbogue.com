<p itemprop="description"><p>I’ve been asked a few times on Google+ how I ‘manage’ my circles.</p>
<p>Here’s how I organize my circles, right now.</p>
<p>I have two circles:</p>
<p><strong>1. ‘Those who have added me’ for everyone who’s followed me.</strong></p>
<p>This first circle just grows. I do a select-all and add using in the circles admin page to add everyone to this.</p>
<p><strong>2. ‘Following’ for people who I’m paying attention to actively. </strong></p>
<p>I add and subtract people from this second circle pretty regularly.</p>
<p>I’m following people who contribute to questions I ask. I’m following people who’s work is blowing my mind. I’m following people who I’ve seen around a lot and want to get to know better. I’m following people who I consider friends around the world.</p>
<p>If you’ve been actively engaging with me and my work, there’s a good chance you’re on this list (or will be soon.)</p>
<p>I haven’t displayed this list publicly because it changes often and <strong>it’s not a popularity contest</strong>.</p>
<p>Google easily allows me to follow everyone back, so I do. When I used Twitter, people often got offended that I didn’t follow them (or unfollowed them). Social media politics make me feel tired, so I just follow everyone back.</p>
<p>I use a <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/gijclepbhbekkghfmcdfkpmbkbefcokp">Chrome plugin that auto-forwards</a> me to my follow list. I tend to hang out in my following circle the most. I do searches on <a href="https://plus.google.com/s/ev%20bogue">people’s names</a> in order to get to know them better (the ‘best of’ is incredibly valuable in getting to know someone) and popular topics in the Google+ search bar.</p>
<p>I’m experimenting with <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223">saving searches for specific people and topics</a> on rotating interest groups as well.</p>
