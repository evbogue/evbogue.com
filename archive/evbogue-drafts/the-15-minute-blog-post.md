<p><i>Written by Ev Bogue | Follow me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a></i></p>
<p>It’s tempting for me to spend all day crafting the perfect post. I want to stress over each syllable of every word. </p>
<p>I want to make it perfect. I want it to land for everyone. I want it to succeed. </p>
<p>Also, I’d also like it to go viral too, while we’re at it.</p>
<p>But then I forget to eat. I forget to go outside. I forget that the coffee got cold an hour ago. I’m hacker-backed and fatigued. </p>
<p>This is why I set deadlines.  This is why I’ve decided on the 15 minute blog post.</p>
<p>One, set a timer for fifteen minutes.</p>
<p>Two, write things.</p>
<p>Three, hit publish.</p>
<p>It seems like a strategy like this would lead to sloppy writing, or poor quality, or both. But it doesn’t. Instead, it forces me to do the work, now. I can’t wait, I can’t procrastinate, I can’t check Google+ five times before I write something. I only have 15 minutes.</p>
<p>Everyone has 15 minutes.</p>
