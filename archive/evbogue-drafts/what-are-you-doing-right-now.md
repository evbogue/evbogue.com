<p itemprop="description"><p>This is a little post about something that occurred to me in regards to why Twitter stopped working.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>Back when Twitter worked for me (before 6 months of confusion, and an <a href="/posts/twitter">untethering</a>) the prompt was:</p>
<p>“What are you doing right now?”</p>
<p>With the rise of new Twitter in December of 2010, the prompt changed to:</p>
<p>“What’s happening?”</p>
<p>I asked both of these questions on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a> to see how different the responses felt.</p>
<p>When I think of “What are you doing right now?” I can answer directly. For example, I’m “Writing a blog post about questions in the nook at Cafe Vita in Seattle.” Or “I’m sipping a half-caff latte, I just made a list of what I need to do today.” Or “I’m battleshipping with +Gwenbell, listening to Kanye West and writing about questions.”</p>
<p>This is <a href="http://www.gwenbell.com/experience-telling">experience telling</a>, it’s telling a story about my work. When we have a millions of people all simultaneously telling stories about themselves, suddenly I have sensory perception of many people’s worlds.</p>
<p>“What’s happening?” Is passive. It’s indirect. It’s the kind of limp question I’d get walking into a hip-hop party in Bushwick back in 2008. “What’s happening bro?”, “Chillin’ yo. Good beats. Where’s the bar?” I can duck behind that question and answer with a non-answer, every single time. It’s okay, because they didn’t expect much of an answer anyway.</p>
<p>Maybe Twitter stopped caring about the answer. That’s why I untethered.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>If you’re asking a question, and you don’t care what the answer is, will it land?</p>
