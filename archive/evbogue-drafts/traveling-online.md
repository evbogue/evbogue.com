<p class="date">October 22, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>As I’ve continued to pack and un-pack my bag over the past few days, wandering down the coast by train from Seattle to San Francisco, I’ve been <a href="/posts/vagabond-things">considering my Things</a>.</p>
<p>What purpose does every Thing serve?</p>
<p>I ask the question <a href="/posts/untethering">“why?”</a> of every Thing that I have. Does it have a good answer? If not. I untether.</p>
<p>I ask these same questions when I travel online. I take a moment to check in. <strong>Where am I now?</strong> Do I want to be here? Where am I hanging out?</p>
<p>I travel instantly online. However, where I travel to can take up endless amounts of time. The days I <a href="/posts/untether-from-facebook">spent in Facebook</a> are days I can never have back. The days I spend creating? They’re valuable from now into the future.</p>
<p>So, I ask the question: why am I here? What have I brought with me? How is it serving me?</p>
<p>Can I answer the question: “why?”</p>
<p>If not. I untether.</p>
