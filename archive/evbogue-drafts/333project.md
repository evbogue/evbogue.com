<p>I&#8217;m not sure where I&#8217;m headed next, but it&#8217;ll be somewhere. I&#8217;ll keep you up to date on <a href="http://www.twitter.com/evbogue">Twitter</a>. A few coffees, great meals, and a beer in the afternoon &#8212; this is all I need.</p>
<p>I get a lot of questions about how I make a full-time income as a writer, so consider this an update.</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
<p>When I was first becoming a writer, the advice I got from professional writers was one of two things.</p>
<p>1. Don&#8217;t be a writer, you&#8217;ll starve.<br/>
2. Work your way up the corporate ladder, send a manuscript to a publisher.</p>
<p>The more I practice being a professional writer in the world, the more I realize that the conventional wisdom about how to be paid to do the craft is all bullshit.</p>
<p>How to be a writer in 2011:</p>
<p>I only need a small group of people to support me. The number I&#8217;m aiming for is 333.</p>
<p>The math is simple:</p>
<p>333 x 25 x 12 = $100,000</p>
<p>333 is the number of people subscribed, a number I&#8217;m closing in on.</p>
<p>$25 is the subscription price per month.</p>
<p>12 is the number of months in a year (hopefully).</p>
<p>Add e-books to this stream of income, and I&#8217;m making a little more.</p>
<p>Then I don&#8217;t need to stress so much about being a writer, experiencing my world, telling it to you.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t need to impress a bazillion people. I don&#8217;t need a book deal. I don&#8217;t need to be featured on the bestsellers table in Barnes and Noble. I don&#8217;t need to answer 333 emails a day from people I don&#8217;t know. I don&#8217;t need an A-list blog with 80,000 people reading it every month. I don&#8217;t need to create a massive organization in order to distribute hand-printed copies of my work.</p>
<p>Why have I been so quiet on the open Net lately? Because&#8230;</p>
<p>I only need 333.</p>
<p>This, for me, is enough.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re a writer, you can do this too.</p>
