<p itemprop="description"><p>The primary way my writing travels is by word of mouth.</p>
<p>There are a couple of ways this happens, these days.</p>
<p>1. Share on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts/">Google+</a>. Then your audience can see who you’re reading, I can stop by and give you a +1! Good curators are valuable on the Net. </p>
<p>2. Forward this to a friend. If you receive the <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">free daily letters</a>, you can always forward the emails to a friend (use the link below, or just forward it using your email client. </p>
<p>3. Link on your blog. Do you blog? Links are what makes the Internet go around. In the olden days, links were all we have. Now we have G+, Facebook and Twitter (if you do those things.) Things change, but links stay the same. They’re the most permanent connection to make.</p>
<p>4. Experiment. Have you come up with an interesting way to spread the word? Drop me an email or engage with me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts/">Google Plus</a>.</p>
<p>I’m also interested to hear: </p>
<p><a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts/1hiEGb2R9kM">How does word spread about your work?</a></p>
