<p class="date">October 23, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>As a publisher online, I often feel like I intuitively ‘know’ what everyone wants to read from me. Most of the time I ask myself: what’s alive in me? And I write that.</p>
<p>Sometimes though, I like to ask a different question. One that is harder for me, but more useful for you.</p>
<p>I ask: <a href="https://evbogue.wufoo.com/forms/what-do-you-want-to-know/">what do you want to know?</a></p>
<p>This tells me more about the needs of my readers. When I ask this question, I get many little answers, which all lead to me knowing a little more about how I can serve my readers better. </p>
<p>This can mean serving my readers better with the invitations that I send out every day on <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">the free daily letter</a>, or in a deeper way on <a href="/posts/evolve">Evolving Your Digital Work</a>. If I get enough questions about one technical topic, I’ll launch a one-month program like I did recently with <a href="/posts/get-paid-to-write">Get Paid to Write</a>.</p>
<p>I can’t answer every question individually (I get a lot), but I listen to every question, so they all find a way to make it into the work over time.</p>
<p>Have you asked what your readers want to know? You’ll be surprised by the answers.</p>
