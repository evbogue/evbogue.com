<p itemprop="description"><p>A few years ago, I started using <a href="http://www.ingdirect.com/">ING Direct</a> as my main bank. </p>
<p>For those of you who aren’t familiar with ING Direct, the premise is simple: One checking account that’s also a savings account. $500 in credit, just in case I overdraft. It’s an Internet bank, so there are only a few locations. They’re part of a shared ATM network, so I have access to cash in many places for free (despite the bank not having physical locations.) There are no hidden fees, and interest is a little higher than your average bank — though nothing special.</p>
<p>Well, recently, <a href="http://moneyland.time.com/2011/06/17/capital-one-buys-ing-direct-and-customers-start-to-freak-out/">Capitol One purchased ING Direct</a>. I’m anticipating that this is as a sign that the awesome simplicity of ING Direct is on it’s way out, as the big bank starts to devour it. </p>
<p>This means that I’m on the market for a new bank.</p>
<p>Priorities for me are: free ATM accessibility in any city (international would be ideal). </p>
<p>What I don’t need: old skool things like paper checks and a million features.</p>
<p>I realize <a href="https://banksimple.com/">Bank Simple</a> is the likely candidate to replace ING Direct as the next awesome simple online bank, but they haven’t launched yet. I’m on their beta list.</p>
<p>If you love your bank (ie, they haven’t done things to screw you over in the last year.) My question to you is this:</p>
<p>What bank do you use? Why do you like it? What benefits are there?</p>
<p>I’d love to hear in the comments.</p>
