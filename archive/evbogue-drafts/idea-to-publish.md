<p itemprop="description"><p>How do I get from idea to publish every day?</p>
<p>It’s easy to stay in idea phase. As Derek Sivers mentions in his book <em><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Anything-You-Want-Derek-Sivers/dp/1936719118">Anything You Want</a></em>: “To me, ideas are worth nothing unless executed. They are just a multiplier. Execution is worth millions.”</p>
<p>So, I do my best to get from idea to execution as fast as possible.</p>
<p>I constantly have an abundance of ideas marinating in my brain at all times. I don’t ever have a problem thinking of new ideas. Even when I was in Seattle for 5 months, which in my experience was a relatively un-stimulating place, I still had an abundance of ideas.</p>
<p>When I move around in the world, I more ideas find themselves in my mind. <strong>My challenge is getting to execution as fast as possible.</strong></p>
<p>This is my process, from idea-to-publish every day:</p>
<ol>
<li>Write a few ideas down in <a href="/posts/how-i-write-in-notebooks">a notebook</a>. I usually do this while I’m eating breakfast, or having a cup of coffee.</li>
<li>Open my Macbook Air, <a href="/posts/working-anywhere">anywhere</a>, and then start writing out the idea as quickly as possible. With the first draft? I don’t worry about spelling, grammar, or even if the idea is any good. I just write.</li>
<li>Read it, after 15-30 minutes. Do I like it? I’m my own harshest critic, so if I don’t, I start over again with the next idea. Sometimes the first three paragraphs will be nonsense, and then I’ll get to the heart of the idea by paragraph 4. Then it’s a simple matter to delete the first three paragraphs. Or, I can always start over.</li>
<li>I give myself a time limit. I have to publish every single day at least once, but usually twice (once to the <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">free daily letter</a>, and then again (with more depth) to one of my premium venues, like <a href="/posts/evolve">Evolving Your Digital Work</a> or <a href="/posts/get-paid-to-write">Get Paid to Write</a>.) This means that I can’t give up. I have to hit publish.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>The pressure to hit publish keeps me on track.</strong> I don’t wait for ‘the perfect idea’.</p>
<p>Idea + execution = the only way I know if my idea will land.</p>
