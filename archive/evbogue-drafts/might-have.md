<p><i>Written by Ev Bogue | Follow me on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">Google+</a></i></p>
<p>“If only I’d done X…”</p>
<p>But I didn’t. </p>
<p>I chose to do what I did in order to get here.</p>
<p>I chose to leave New York two years ago, and every time I’ve gone back I haven’t been there while I was gone (because I was somewhere else.) I chose to leave San Francisco in February. These are the facts, nothing can change that, because the past has been written — it’s immutable. I can’t time travel back and manipulate what my past-self should have done, time travel doesn’t work that way.</p>
<p>I can reach back into the past and learn from what happened. I can reach back and embrace it, because only by embracing what I choose can I feel okay with how I got here.</p>
<p>Spending one moment more than necessary worrying about what I should have done yesterday is a moment that I’m not spending now taking concrete actions that are necessary in order to achieve what I need now.</p>
<p>What might have happened never happened. </p>
<p>So, I untether.</p>
