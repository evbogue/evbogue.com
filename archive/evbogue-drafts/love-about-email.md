<p class="date">November 12, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>Yesterday I asked a question: what technologies have become obsolete for you? I asked you to email me.</p>
<p>I got so many amazing responses from that message. It reminded me why I love to get emails. </p>
<p>Part of this is the recent Gmail upgrade, which makes entering my inbox so much more rewarding than it used to be. I get to see your faces. I love that, it makes me feel like I’m more connected with the people who gather around my work.</p>
<p>Another element of receiving good emails in response to my <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">free daily letters</a> is asking good questions. If I ask the wrong questions, I get the wrong answers, and my energy starts to feel taxed. I want to feel enlivened by the emails I received. This is why I try to ask opening questions.</p>
<p>A question like “what technologies have you untethered from?” fills my inbox with new knowledge. I begin to see that some of you have untethered from your iPhones, others have left behind Flickr, others decided they didn’t want to use Tumblr anymore.</p>
<p>I know this would be hard to achieve, but what if we lived in a world where ever email was a joy to receive? I find that asking opening questions helps make that possible.</p>
<p>-Ev</p>
