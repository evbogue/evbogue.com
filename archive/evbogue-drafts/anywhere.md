<p>One of the most important elements of working online is transparency, so I put together this page to show you how I make money online.</p>
<p>I hope that this page can simultaneously give you some of your own ideas on how you can support yourself in a location independent manner.</p>
<p><strong>I&#8217;m an author.</strong></p>
<p>Initially, I wrote an e-book called <em>The Art of Being Minimalist</em> (now out of print) that taught people how to get rid of their stuff. I made around $40,000 a year selling this e-book over the Internet to readers of my blog &#8212; now I make more. In February of 2011, I switched out <em>The Art of Being Minimalist</em> for my book on <em><a href="http://ebookling.com/items/augmented-humanity-second-selves-mental-cybernetics-and-the-future-of-you?ref=TCUJSWPo">Augmented Humanity</a></em>, which shows a deeper story about why minimalism is happening to you: humans and machines are becoming indistinguishable. Sounds crazy? Read and find out. If you like the work here, I&#8217;d love if you&#8217;d support my work by delving deeper.</p>
<p>A good free introduction to my writing is <em><a href="http://ebookling.com/items/how-to-create-a-movement-20--2?ref=TCUJSWPo">How to Create a Movement</a></em>.</p>
<p><strong>I write for a small group of dedicated readers.</strong></p>
<p>Information travels much faster in the modern age, and it&#8217;s flowing faster. Different information flows have different prices. The blog is free, and the content comes, goes, and grows. An e-book is a timeless repository of ideas that apply through space/time in a consistent manner. I realized recently that I needed a commitment from my readers before I dropped them into the deepest work that I&#8217;m doing, so I set up a paid subscription Letter.ly where I publish my most important and valuable work. Some of this work is exclusive to the Letter, and won&#8217;t be published elsewhere. Some of it eventually makes it to the blog, if it can be revised for public consumption. Subscribe to the <a href="http://www.letter.ly/evbogue">Letter.ly here</a>.</p>
<p><strong>I evolve your digital work.</strong></p>
<p>After I wrote <em>The Art of Being Minimalist</em>, I received hundreds of emails asking how I was able to make a living running a simple business online.  In May 2009, I wrote <em><a href="http://ebookling.com/items/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere?ref=TCUJSWPo">Minimalist Business</a></em> to answer these questions and give you a glimpse into what your own future could be. You can learn how to run a zero-overhead online business through experimentation, or you can learn how I was able to do it by reading the e-book. Occasionally I give people a business evolutionary boost over Skype. If you&#8217;re interested in a 1-on-1, <a href="http://evbogue.us2.list-manage.com/subscribe?u=19b7e7d1ae6d6a19aff999ed3&amp;id=3d91c99865">sign up here to be notified</a> when a slot is available &#8212; supplies are limited and I&#8217;m very selective with who I work with at this point. Please read <em><a href="http://ebookling.com/items/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere?ref=TCUJSWPo">Minimalist Business</a></em> first, so we can get on the same page.</p>
<p>All of these income streams combine to a living wage that&#8217;s much higher than your average job.</p>
<p><strong>Why I&#8217;m able to live anywhere.</strong></p>
<p>I still have near zero-overhead, I work infrequently and when I want to. My e-book sales provide a base level of income for comfort. At this point, the idea of working at a desk seems bizarre to me &#8212; though I&#8217;ve been told some people like it. I&#8217;m able to do this because my life overhead is low. I don&#8217;t need much to make me happy. I don&#8217;t own or want <a href="/posts/things">many things</a>. I drink coffee and smoothies, I practice yoga, occasionally I enjoy a well-crafted beer. I don&#8217;t own a car, and wherever I may be located I rent or share a small apartment. I live my life in the world. This means while my income is significant, it also doesn&#8217;t matter much. I&#8217;m able to explore creative angles without much risk by living with less.</p>
<p><strong>My location independent office.</strong></p>
<p>I don&#8217;t have an office. Instead I work in-between on my 11&#8243; MacBook air and an iPhone. You&#8217;ll find me in coffee shops in Boulder, San Francisco, New York, Chicago, or elsewhere in the world writing and enjoying a single origin Americano. To see where I am now, follow on <a href="http://www.twitter.com/evbogue">Twitter</a>. I&#8217;m able to do this because I run a <em><a href="http://ebookling.com/items/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere?ref=TCUJSWPo">Minimalist Business</a></em>. I don&#8217;t have to work out of obligation. Instead, I work on what interests me most at any moment in time. I commonly jump out of bed in the morning excited for life. If I don&#8217;t want to work, I don&#8217;t &#8212; but most days I really do!
