<p><em>November 5th, 2011. Nayarit, Mexico.</em></p>
<p>“I am going to launch something.”</p>
<p>I know this, because I’ve just said it. I’m sitting at a cafe, eating eggs and tomatoes.</p>
<p>I asked across the table: “what do you want to know from me?”</p>
<p>“I want to know how you do launches so effortlessly.”</p>
<p>“I hate writing about launches.”</p>
<p>“I know you do, but that’s exactly why you need to launch this. Because you told me if I keep answering questions that annoy the shit out of me, that means I have a potential launch on my hands. And the launch questions I ask you annoy the shit out of you.”</p>
<p>Now? I’m sitting in another cafe. I’ve just received my ‘jugo verde’, and I’m sitting down. I’m going to write the sales page today.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>I don’t like launches. They’re hard. Launches are bringing something into the world, and everything I bring into the world I know it will eventually die. Oh, and while it’s alive? Some people will hate it. Simultaneously, I realize that launches really help people. It gives them an opportunity to learn, grow – go deeper.</p>
<p>The benefit to me? Launches give me an opportunity to make a whole lot of money. Then? I can afford to travel around and enjoy jugo verde.</p>
<p>The financial goal for this launch? $17,395. Ideally, I’ll make this in one day.</p>
<p>I know it’ll be a little less than that, because of taxes and Amazon Payments. I don’t focus on how it’ll be a little less. I think about the total incoming number, right now. I write it down. I frame it in my mind.</p>
<p>Launch day? It’s Tuesday, November 8th, 2011. That’s when the sales page goes up (what I’m writing now, and what you’re reading now.)</p>
<p>Ship day? That’s Monday, November 14th, 2011. How long will the project last? Every single day for 5 days. Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday.</p>
<p>I don’t get a day off during this launch. Then again, neither do you.</p>
<p>I’ve decided <strong>only 35 people will be part of this launch</strong>.<br/>
Only 35 people will know what I’m going to write.<br/>
<strong>Each one of these 35 people will pay $497</strong> for what I’m going to write.</p>
<p><strong>Less than $100 a day for a 5-day intensive plunge experience isn’t a lot of money.</strong></p>
<p>I’m listening to Kill Hannah on my headphones. Why? Because they’re the band that I used to listen to as a late teenager, jumping up and down in Chicago. For some reason, listening to Kill Hannah helps me launch.</p>
<p>I don’t ask questions, I just listen as I’m writing this launch page.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p><strong>The first rule of launching?</strong> You’re going to get this for free.</p>
<p>The reason I’m giving it to you for free is because it’s the most important aspect of any launch. I hope that you share this with everyone you know. Why? Because it’s my ultimate pet peeve about launches. Pay attention.</p>
<h4>I only launch to people who have given me permission.</h4>
<p>This is why I gather permission every day. I do this on <a href="http://www.evbogue.com/">Evbogue.com</a>. I do this on Google+. I do this at the end of every email I send out. I say: do you want to <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">sign up for free daily letters</a>?</p>
<p>Have you ever been pitched on something you don’t need? It happens to me all the time.</p>
<p>Here are some poor ways to launch that will make people hate you.</p>
<ol>
<li>Launch on social media, Facebook, Twitter, Google+.</li>
<li>Email people (especially friends!) who didn’t sign up for your list.</li>
<li>Walk up to people on the beach and pitch them on your didgeridoo music, then tell them they’re heartless when they say “no.”</li>
<li>Launch something that no one needs.</li>
<li>Launch something that only fills the needs of the person shipping, not the person on the receiving end.</li>
</ol>
<p>If you haven’t gathered permission, you’re violating someone’s personal space.</p>
<p>Seth Godin wrote a book in 1999 that changed my life. It’s called <em><a href="http://www.amazon.com/Permission-Marketing-Turning-Strangers-Customers/dp/0684856360/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1320540302&sr=8-1">Permission Marketing</a></em>. I want to get a copy shipped to Mexico, so I can smack Didgeridude over the head with it the next time I see him. However, they tell me there’s no postal service here.</p>
<p>Permission Marketing taught me this: the single most important rule of launches.</p>
<p><strong>You <em>MUST</em> <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">gather permission</a> before you launch a Thing.</strong></p>
<p>//</p>
<p>If you’re receiving this, <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">you’ve given me permission to contact you</a>. If you haven’t given me permission, then you’ve either clicked in from a page that someone shared, or you clicked a link on my blog to get here.</p>
<p>If you’re here because you gave me permission to contact you, and don’t want to hear from me again? Unsubscribe from the <a href="http://eepurl.com/eKhvg">free daily emails</a>.</p>
<p>If you want to be part of this small group of people who are going to learn how to Launch Things, you’ll need to come up with $497 before 35 other people sign up for Launch Things.</p>
<p>This could fill up today. You will never know.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>I don’t particularly want to talk about launches. However, it is an important topic, and pivotal to making money online. I’m getting so many requests for this, and it’s annoying me – so I decided to turn it into a launch. Will you be one of the 35?</p>
<p>The likelihood of me doing this again? Low.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>It’s 8:50 am on Saturday. The clouds are clearing over the rainforest just beyond the town square. I’ve finished my jugo verde. I’m ready to push publish. I create a page in WordPress. I design artwork. I set up a new list in Mailchimp. I set it to charge a one-time fee of $497. I’ll keep an eye on the list, to make sure it doesn’t exceed 35 people.</p>
<p>Now, I have to put one final question to you.</p>
<p>Do you want to learn how to Launch Things?</p>
<p>If you do, here’s the buy now button. I hope you click it.</p>

However, I decided not to since I’ve gathered permission from 2,800+ people, <strong>I expect that 35 people will sign up for this very quickly</strong>.]</p>
<p>I’ll see you on the inside,<br/>
Ev</p>
<p>Giving this one a miss? Don’t worry, I’ll be launching more Things soon.</p>
