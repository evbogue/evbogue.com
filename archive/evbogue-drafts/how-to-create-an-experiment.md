<p class="date">October 13, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>Creating an experiment is easy. Measuring it is hard.</p>
<p>Every action that I take in the digital space, I consider an experiment. </p>
<p>If I’m not experimenting, I’m either assuming or not doing the work. Assuming is when I read something that someone tells me to do and take it as a fact. Not doing the work is avoiding doing an experiment, because I’m afraid it will fail (or will be too hard.)</p>
<p>Here are the 3 steps toward creating an experiment.</p>
<p>1. Write down your intention for the experiment, and a time frame.</p>
<p>Ex: “Write every day on my blog for 30 days.”</p>
<p>2. Decide how you’re <a href="/posts/are-you-measuring">measuring</a>.</p>
<p>Ex: “In order to attract 20 more paying customers to <a href="/posts/evolve">my Letter</a>.”</p>
<p>3. Check results.</p>
<p>After 30 days, I check measurable sources to see if I’ve been successful. <a href="http://eepurl.com/fs1tY">Mailchimp</a> [Rewards link] is a great way to measure. <a href="/posts/earn">Income</a> is another great way. </p>
<p>If success: repeat experiment with bigger criteria.<br/>
If fail: It’s time to untether.</p>
</p>
