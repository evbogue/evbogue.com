<p class="date">October 11, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>I used to do a thing called ‘hype’. Hype is short for hypothetical.</p>
<p>I wrote in depth about this in a sample Letter from <a href="/posts/unthering-from-hype">Evolving Your Digital Work: Untethering from Hype</a>.</p>
<p>These days, I try to avoid hype in my writing and communications as much as possible. I do this because when I use hype, I’m setting expectations about where or what I’ll be doing in the future. If I end up not doing what I said I was going to do, then I’ve set expectations and not met them.</p>
<p>This is particularly problematic when I say I’m going to do something extraordinary. When I say I’m going to do something extraordinary, then I’ve created a promise to you that I’ll do something. “I’m going to do something… soon!”</p>
<p>If I end up doing something else, then there’s confusion. </p>
<p>Then, I’m in a situation where I’ve broken my promise to myself and to you.</p>
</p>
