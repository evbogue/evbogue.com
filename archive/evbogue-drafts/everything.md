<p class="date">October 18, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>I’m writing this on a train, rocketing down the west coast from Seattle to San Francisco. </p>
<p>Almost everyone in the observation car is snapping photos and videos on their cellphones as the train travels by a large body of water. Maybe they’ll push those photos and videos to the web later? It’s only a matter of time before we’ve captured everything, maybe we already have captured this view from this train a million times from thousands of perspectives.</p>
<p>If we have captured everything from every angle, does that begin to change how we capture? </p>
<p>Is it my original capture, my experience, what makes this ride different from everyone else’s?</p>
<p>These are the questions arising as I make my way down the west coast.</p>
<p>I don’t know where I’m going, but I do know I’ll let you know when I get there.</p>
