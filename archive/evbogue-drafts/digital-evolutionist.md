<p class="date">October 16, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>A digital evolutionist assists people in embracing new technological systems as they come into existence on the web.</p>
<p><strong>The digital world is constantly changing.</strong> Books don’t even stay the same anymore. Take a moment and think back to last year, how different was it? Technology, and thus the web is said to be evolving at <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/ray_kurzweil_on_how_technology_will_transform_us.html">an exponential rate</a>. </p>
<p>We need people who are able to sit at the edge, observe the ebb and flow of technology, and report back about what is happening. A digital evolutionist is a map maker, but not of geography — instead a digital evolutionist maps the edge of the digital space as it grows and changes. </p>
<p>In order to stay on the cutting edge of digital technologies, a digital evolutionist untethers from old technologies as they become obsolete. A digital evolutionist sees every technology they use as <a href="/posts/how-to-create-an-experiment">an experiment</a>. Many services and communities on the web have come and gone since I started publishing online. Many more will come and go as we move into the future.</p>
<p>It’s a digital evolutionist’s job to assess wether technologies, new or old, are working. If they’re not? A digital evolutionist <a href="http://evbogue.com/category/untether/">untethers</a>.</p>
</p>
