<p itemprop="description"><p>“Do You Need to Promote Your Writing?”</p>
<p>Thom asked this question in a recent <a href="http://www.themicropublisher.com/do-you-need-to-promote-your-writing/">blog post at The Micropublisher</a>. I answered with this <a href="https://plus.google.com/103294243982017985401/posts/5CDLneTTxSh">comment on his Google+ stream</a>. </p>
<p>“How do I push words?” is a question that I’ve asked myself recently.</p>
<p>I’ve tried a number of experiments over the last year.</p>
<p>For a number of months, earlier this year, I retreated from the public. I went to a metaphorical cabin in the woods and only wrote what I truly thought mattered. Then I put my work up, and hoped that it would find its way to the people that it would touch.</p>
<p>I found that I wasn’t reaching enough people this way. My audience kind of plateaued during this reclusive phase. <strong>Being a recluse was beneficial</strong>, because it allowed me to turn internal, and focus on the work and the edit. I cleaned up my writing, learned to tell from experience, and rediscovered alignment in myself and my work.</p>
<p>By August, I realized that it was time to bring more people into my work. So, I began reaching out, marketing to the world again. As Thom referred to it: promoting.</p>
<p>Marketing isn’t easy. I used to believe that the way to market my work was to bomb social media with my blog posts. Over time, I realized that this didn’t work as well as I thought it did. So, I stopped do this.</p>
<p>Instead of pushing, I listen. Instead of promoting, I linchpin discussions around the work.</p>
<p>As in many things, I see this work as an experiment in using strategies. <strong>If a strategy doesn’t work, I untether from it.</strong></p>
<p>//</p>
<p><strong>How are you bringing your words to the world?</strong> I’d love to hear the strategies that are working for you in the comments.</p>
<p>If the strategies you’re using aren’t working, say what you’ve tried, and maybe we can suggest some alternatives.</p>
