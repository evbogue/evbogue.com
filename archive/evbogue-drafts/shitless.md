<p><a href="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5029/5596206194_fcfb5ed47a.jpg"><img class="alignright" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5029/5596206194_fcfb5ed47a.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300"/></a>Looking back at everything I&#8217;ve done that I thought would destroy me, nothing ever has.</p>
<p>Before I did them? They scared the shit out of me&#8230;</p>
<ul>
<li>Quitting my job at New York Magazine in July of 2009 scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Leaving the Schoolhouse scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Moving to Portland in August of 2009 scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Living on $3,000 for three months scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Writing my first e-book scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Going back to New York after living in Portland scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Writing a <a href="http://ebookling.com/items/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere?ref=TCUJSWPo">business e-book</a> scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Moving to Oakland scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Taking a month-long digital sabbatical scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Breaking up with Alix scared the shit out of me (before we did it.)</li>
<li>Moving to Oakland alone (and mainly the living alone part) scared the shit of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Being featured on national news scared the shit of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Teaching yoga scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Accidentally finding myself in Boulder Colorado scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Writing a <a href="/posts/augmented-humanity">book about cyborgs</a> scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Destroying Far Beyond The Stars (my old blog) scared the shit out of me (before I did it.)</li>
<li>Writing within the intimacy of the <a href="http://www.letterly.net/evbogue">letter</a> scares the shit out of me every time (before I do it.)</li>
</ul>
<p>Just about every extraordinary thing I&#8217;ve ever done scared me shitless before I did it. It seemed alright while I was doing it. Afterwards, it seemed like cake.</p>
<p>Every single thing on that list I could do in a heartbeat, if I wanted to, again. It wouldn&#8217;t scare the shit out of me, I know that it wouldn&#8217;t. Thinking about doing any of those things doesn&#8217;t challenge me anymore.</p>
<ul>
<li>Quitting email scares the shit out of me (but I&#8217;ll do it.)</li>
<li>Speaking in Melbourne scares the shit out of me (but I&#8217;ll do it.)</li>
<li>Launching a new blog always scares the shit out of me (but I&#8217;ll do it.)</li>
<li>Talking about things that might unseat a lot of people&#8217;s beliefs (but are true for many others) scares the shit out of me (but I&#8217;ll do it.)</li>
</ul>
<p>What doesn&#8217;t scare us shitless? What everyone else wants us to do. The art of conformity: doing what we&#8217;re told.</p>
<p>So, I say, ignore everybody. They&#8217;re scared shitless too.</p>
<p>What do you want to do that scares the shit out of you? I say do it.</p>
<p>It won&#8217;t be so scary afterwards. I promise.</p>
