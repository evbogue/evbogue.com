<p><a href="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5024/5598457643_68f04a5531.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="http://farm6.static.flickr.com/5024/5598457643_68f04a5531.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300"/></a>I&#8217;ve recently become obsessed with James Franco.</p>
<p>He&#8217;s someone who creates an insane amount of work, while doing everything. I create an insane amount of work, but it&#8217;s nothing compared to what this guy does. He seems to walk the line between creativity and insanity. There&#8217;s no difference between his life and his career. He&#8217;s appears to be either unstoppable or completely unstable.</p>
<p>I believe that by studying completely insane people who are wildly successful, we can learn a lot. This is why I decided to break down James Franco&#8217;s success into 5 simple bullet points. That way we can pretend to be like him by doing things he does, and in the process perhaps we&#8217;ll end up being 1/5th as insanely successful as he is at being him.</p>
<p><strong>Here are the 5 defining characteristics that make James Franco way more successful than you and I:</strong></p>
<p>These are all based off of the piece that Sam Anderson did on Franco in May of last year for New York Magazine &#8212; that I just read now &#8212; <a href="http://nymag.com/movies/profiles/67284/">Is James Franco for Real?</a>.</p>
<p>Sam and I did not know each other personally at New York Magazine, we did pass in the halls at times as we were getting lots of things done.</p>
<p>Franco and I did not know each other personally at NYU, but we did pass each other on 2nd Avenue once at 1am years ago.</p>
<p><strong>1. He&#8217;s keenly aware that he&#8217;s going to die.</strong></p>
<p>&#8220;Little James burst into tears. He was inconsolable. Eventually, he managed to choke out, between sobs, “But I don’t want to die! I have so much to do!”&#8221;</p>
<p>Death is a powerful motivator. Many people go about life pretending like they&#8217;re going to live forever. Well, you&#8217;re not. You&#8217;re going to die. Looking at yourself in the mirror and whispering the words: &#8220;you&#8217;re going to die, get off your ass and make some work.&#8221; is a powerful way to do lots of things with your life. If you aren&#8217;t already aware that you&#8217;re going to die, maybe it&#8217;s time to ask yourself that question.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t create a bucket list, instead actually do things.</p>
<p><strong>2. He has a personal assistant who feeds him.</strong></p>
<p>&#8220;Morgan, a former UCLA classmate of Franco’s, manages his minute-to-minute existence: makes sure he wakes up, gets dressed, eats. “I guarantee you he would not eat unless I fed him,” she says.&#8221;</p>
<p>I&#8217;m constantly forgetting to eat, and so is Franco apparently. When I don&#8217;t eat, I end up being unproductive and grumpy. Sometimes I start crying and I&#8217;m not sure why. This is because highly active people like James Franco burn through calories like crazy while they&#8217;re jumping around, drinking coffee, and generally getting important things done.</p>
<p><strong>3. His sexuality is an open question.</strong></p>
<p>&#8220;Many people are obsessed—and Franco has given them ample reason to be—with the question of whether he’s gay or straight.&#8221;</p>
<p>We&#8217;d all do well to walk the gender line a little closer than we do. Do you feel gayer than you are? Well, act that way. It&#8217;ll make you more successful like James Franco. Just don&#8217;t ever answer the question if you&#8217;re asked flat out whether you make out with girls or boys. Are you pansexual or polyamorous? Still no answer. Let everyone talk about it at the dining room table, it&#8217;ll generate hype for your immersive career.</p>
<p>Would I have a threesome with James and a steaming hot not-to-be-named here beautiful woman? I can&#8217;t answer that.</p>
<p><strong>4. He knows you can&#8217;t pick a career anymore.</strong></p>
<p>You can&#8217;t just be an actor anymore, or a blogger for that matter. You have to be your life, and your life needs to broadcast across all mediums. We&#8217;re living in the age of immersion. We&#8217;re all performance artists to some extent, performing our lives for the world. Do you want to act in a movie, direct a movie, write a book, write a movie, and get four graduate degrees all at the same time? Well, that&#8217;s what it takes to be successful now. You better hire someone to feed you while you&#8217;re doing all of that.</p>
<p><strong>5. Lots of caffeine and pushing publish.</strong></p>
<p>I&#8217;m cracked out on Boulder Teahouse chai right now, so I&#8217;m just going to publish this even though I never write about celebrities.</p>
<p>James Franco doesn&#8217;t care what you think about him, he doesn&#8217;t care whether you think he&#8217;s gay or straight. He doesn&#8217;t care if you think he&#8217;s a bombing artist or an amateur artist. He simply pushes publish on everything he does, regardless of what someone&#8217;s going to think about it. Does that mean that sometimes he releases something that isn&#8217;t perfect? Hell yes.</p>
<p>Meanwhile, his assistant is getting him another quad-shot latte and a sandwich. Here, eat this James, before you pass out.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s summarize: You&#8217;re going to die. Have someone feed you. Am I gay? Maybe. Immersion is the only medium. Caffeine and pressing the publish button no matter how gross.</p>
