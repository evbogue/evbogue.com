<p itemprop="description"><p>Here’s a rule that I’ve made for myself:</p>
<p>If I think I’ve created something that’s worth $5. I give it away.</p>
<p><strong>Here’s why I give away $5 ideas:</strong> the math never works out on a $5 product or service.</p>
<p>If I get 150 people to sign up for my $5 product this month, I’ve only made $750.</p>
<p>If I get 150 people to pay $5 for my product monthly for a year? That’s only $9000.</p>
<p>How many people do I need paying me $5 a month to make $100,00 a year? 1,666. In my experience of doing the work online, that’s a lot of people.</p>
<p>If I’ve created something that’s worth $5. I’m better off giving it away for free.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>If I believe something is worth $5, I either scrap it or give it away.</p>
<p>Then I create something worth $37 or $557. </p>
<p>If I get 150 people to buy a product at $37, I’ve just made $5,550.<br/>
If I get 25 people to buy a product at $557, I’ve just made $13,925</p>
<p>Now, I can focus on less people (with more money on hand) to support me.</p>
<p>This way I can focus on a few clients who have money, instead of a lot of clients who don’t have much to spend.</p>
<p>How much is this idea worth to you?</p>
