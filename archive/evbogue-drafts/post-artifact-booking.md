<h4>Change + Books</h4>
<p>How we’re designing, distributing, and even consuming, books is changing.</p>
<p>Up until recently, we thought about books as the artifact of an act that happened in near isolation.</p>
<p>A writer created silently at a cabin in the woods. He got up the courage to send his finished manuscript off to a publishing house. There the writer was either rejected or accepted. Then they sent back the manuscript and said: change this.</p>
<p>Creating books no longer works this way. </p>
<p>Now we see books evolve through real time. The edits happen between audience and author, maybe even long before a book is published. The work happens in the relationships between author and reader.</p>
<p><strong>Questions in my mind: </strong></p>
<p>What formats will we use? EPUB3 (with it’s intergration with CSS3) seems to be the upcoming standard, but that doesn’t mean PDF won’t be around for awhile.</p>
<p>How can I allow readers to interact with book material during the early creation process? (Is The Letter best good for this?)</p>
<p>When I look at the publishing world, I see many adapting slowly — because they don’t know what to do yet.</p>
<p>And of course, I’m experimenting myself. <em>-Ev</em></p>
