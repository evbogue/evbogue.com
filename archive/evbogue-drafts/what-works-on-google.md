<p itemprop="description"><p>This is the second part in my two-part series on how I’m using Google+ in an effective way.</p>
<p>The first part was about how to <a href="/posts/why-i-google-with-beginners-mind">use Google+ with beginner’s mind</a>.</p>
<p>I’ve been using Google+ for two months now. As I said in the last post: If a method of using a service falls flat a few times, I untether. Why keep doing something if it isn’t working?</p>
<p>So, what works (for me) on Google+?</p>
<p><strong>1. Linchpinning.</strong> I’ve started asking a question a day, and then I get out of the way. I don’t state an opinion. I don’t philosophize. I just ask a question, and let the people who are interested in my stream say what is alive in them. Eventually, these people begin following each other, strengthening the network around me and their work. </p>
<p><strong>2. Interviews.</strong> Plus a person in public or private and ask a specific question aimed at opening a conversation. My <a href="/posts/normal-human-experienc">interview with Gwen Bell</a> on experience telling was done on Google+. In my experience, this is an effortless way to work on a good Interview with someone you admire.</p>
<p><strong>3. Mentions.</strong> When I come across a stream where I can mention someone, I do. I could say: “well, as <a href="https://plus.google.com/116416314233992548280/posts">+Kevin Kelly</a> said in <a href="http://www.kk.org/thetechnium/archives/2011/04/techno_life_ski.php">Techno Life Skills</a>: ‘The habits of twitter won’t work in what is next.’” Then, Kevin Kelly knows that I’ve mentioned him. Some of my followers follow KK. Our network grows stronger as my audience and his audience begin to get familiar with each other’s work. </p>
<p><strong>4. Sharing.</strong> If I come across something that blows my mind, I share it on Google+. I don’t do this every day. The work has to be very high quality. This means that I’ve only shared a few things on Google+ so far over the last two months. I don’t want to waste the time of the people who follow me with stuff that isn’t blowing my own mind.</p>
