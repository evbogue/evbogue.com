<p itemprop="description"><p>For the month of August, I published every single day to <a href="http://www.evbogue.com/">Evbogue.com</a>.</p>
<p>I wrote six letters to <a href="/posts/evolve">Evolving Your Digital Work</a>.</p>
<p>I launched <a href="/posts/taking-your-book-to-the-web">Taking Your Book to the Web</a>: a 12 week project aimed at teaching a small group of people (151, <a href="/posts/selling-out">in the end</a>) the skills they need to bring their book into this online space.</p>
<p>Looking back, I see progress. That’s the amazing thing about writing every single day. I feel my writing opening up to new possibilities. </p>
<p>Simultaneously, I’ve <a href="/posts/backup-plans-are-bullshit">untethered from my past work</a> during the month of August. It was time to move on, to new things and new work.</p>
<p>Untethering from past work leaves me sitting in a space of uncertainty. I’ve been hearing that word a lot lately, I hope that we know what we’re describing when we use it. </p>
<p>Uncertainty, for me, is a feeling of being alone, scared, and not-knowing. Uncertainty is the moment before fear sets in. Uncertainty can be a powerful motivator for getting things done. In my experience, I can’t develop strategies to exist in uncertainty, but sitting in it long enough (and practicing in it, on the mat), I start to see the strategies that will pull me towards the next work that I need to do. </p>
<p>This isn’t escaping uncertainty.</p>
<p>In fact, in my experience, there is no escaping uncertainty. The only way to try is to pad myself against it: I want to escape that uneasy feeling, so I start drinking too much, or a eat too much, or a begin watching too much Hulu. That’s trying to escape uncertainty, and it’s only at temporary relief at best. </p>
<p>Eventually, uncertainty catches up to even the most reclusive couch-dweller.</p>
<p>There is no escape from uncertainty — the future has always looked uncertain, because it hasn’t been written yet. This is good, because it means I have a choices to make in the future.</p>
<p>So I sit in the anxiety of not-knowing. Eventually, I stumble across ideas, I execute on those, and find myself in the deeper work. Hopefully that resonates with you.</p>
