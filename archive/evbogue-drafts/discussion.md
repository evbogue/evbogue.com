<p itemprop="description"><p>My free daily letter doesn’t have comments. I <a href="/posts/untethered-comments">untethered from them</a>.</p>
<p>However, that doesn’t need to stop you from having a discussion around the content. </p>
<p>You can always link in to the post from your own blog. </p>
<p>Here’s another idea: why not gather a discussion on your own <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts">Google+</a> page?</p>
<p>If I find an idea or a blog post that I want to discuss in more depth, here’s what I do.</p>
<p>1. Share the URL on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts">Google+</a>. </p>
<p>2. Ask underneath: <a href="/posts/how-was-your-experience">how was your experience?</a></p>
<p>3. Comment with my experience on Google+ under that shared item.</p>
<p>You can do this on another network too. I’m using Google+ here as an example, because it’s the only social network that I’m currently a part of. Google+ is also very good for starting gatherings of this kind, because the services promotes gathering for discussions.</p>
<p>Bonus, if you want to notify me that discussion is taking place, add me to the discussion by using <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/">+Ev Bogue</a> anywhere on the post. I’ll drop by if I can!</p>
