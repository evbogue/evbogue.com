<p>Venice Beach, LA. February 12th 2011.</p>
<p><a href="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4078/5436500053_84a42d5e56_m.jpg"><img alt="" src="http://farm5.static.flickr.com/4078/5436500053_84a42d5e56_m.jpg" class="alignleft" width="300" height="300"/></a>Over the last year+, I moved through various states of location independence.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s a brief timeline of where I traveled through space/time:</p>
<p>Portland, OR August &#8211; November 2009<br/>
Chicago, IL November &#8211; January 2010<br/>
Brooklyn, NY January &#8211; May 2010<br/>
Oakland, CA May &#8211; October 2010<br/>
San Francisco, CA November &#8211; December 2011</p>
<p>&#8230;and then suddenly I&#8217;ve gone full blown nomadic (for the first time.)</p>
<p>Chicago, IL Dec 24th &#8211; Jan 4th<br/>
New York, NY Jan 4th &#8211; 20th<br/>
Boulder, CO Jan 20th &#8211; February 11th</p>
<p>I&#8217;m in LA now, February 11th-February 12th.</p>
<p>Projected schedule right now looks like:</p>
<p>San Francisco &#8211;&gt; Chicago &#8211;&gt; Boulder &#8211;&gt; Austin &#8211;&gt; Boulder &#8211;&gt; Chicago (by May 7th) &#8211;&gt; Melbourne, Australia</p>
<p>When I change surroundings, my experience of time seems to expand. January in NY seemed like a year. Boulder seemed like 10 years. I&#8217;m not sure if everyone&#8217;s experience of time is changing in this way, or if it&#8217;s just me and the people who surround me.</p>
<p>Most days I feel like more happens than did for years in my past.</p>
<p>After being on the road since December, I can&#8217;t even remember what is still in my room in San Francisco. Physical objects hold no powerful for me. I believe this is related to expanding mental cybernetic capacities, combined delving deeper into my own true nature.</p>
<p>If you left San Francisco for 11 years, which were in reality only 1.5 months, would you remember?</p>
