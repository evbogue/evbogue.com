<p>I get asked this a lot:</p>
<p>“Why would you ever…?”</p>
<p>Leave a city that you love so much.<br/>
Delete a best selling e-book that still sells copies every day.<br/>
Quit a job with a fancy title.<br/>
Stop eating something that tastes good.<br/>
Donate a perfectly good jacket because you I have two.</p>
<p>Why? Because it isn’t a challenge anymore. I’m covering the same ground again and again. I’m taking 25% of my energy and investing it in time that has already eclipsed. That leaves me with only 75% for the new stuff. As more things build up in the past, more energy gets dedicated to maintenance. Pretty soon I’m maintaining with 100%, which equals stagnation.</p>
<p>I don’t want to maintain connections, I want to grow the existing, or create new ones. This applies to everything in life.</p>
<p>I untether to evolve.</p>
