<p itemprop="description"><p>I asked this question on <a href="https://plus.google.com/116298207273891385223/posts/">Google+</a>: What would you do with $100,000 in the bank? (It was a private question, so some of you might not be able to see it.)</p>
<p>The reason I asked was because someone asked me the same question, and I couldn’t come up with a good answer. I hope that I can come up with a decent answer at some point at the future, but right now I’m existing in the uncertain space of not knowing what I’d do if I had $100,000 in the bank. </p>
<p>Maybe that’s because I’m doing exactly what I want with my life: drinking a toddie on what might be the last day of summer in Seattle, writing things in Scrivener. Or perhaps I don’t have a very good imagination.</p>
<p>What the Google+ thread brought up in me though was a question: how many of these things could be done right now? </p>
<p>Here are some of the things that people wanted to do with $100,000:<br/>
- Start a company<br/>
- Teach yoga<br/>
- Drop out of school<br/>
- Eat real food<br/>
- Travel<br/>
- Buy things for family<br/>
- Start a vegan food cart<br/>
- Move somewhere<br/>
- Go on a cruise<br/>
- Sell a house<br/>
- Hire people<br/>
- Build Apps<br/>
- Quit my job<br/>
- Make art<br/>
- Get married<br/>
- Become a writer</p>
<p>The two things that definitely required $100,000 were these:<br/>
- Saving $50,000<br/>
- Investing in $10,000 in 10 startups.<br/>
- Pay down debt (consumer, student loan, mortgages)</p>
<p>Surprisingly absent from this list was anyone saying: buy more stuff. Maybe this is just the crowd I attract though.</p>
<p>Now, I don’t want to downplay how nice it’d be to have $100,000 in the bank. This seems like a worthwhile dream for me and you. It would give me some extra padding just in case something happened. </p>
<p>My question is this: Do you really need to wait until you have $100,000 to do what you want with your life?</p>
<p>Based on the list of answers above, I’m not sure we need to wait.</p>
