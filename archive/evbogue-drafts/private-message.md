<p class="date">October 4, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p>I get a lot of questions about how to message one person on Google+, so I thought I’d write a short post on how to plus only one person on Google+.</p>
<h4>Choosing a circle</h4>
<p>Whenever I share a message on Google+, I have to make a choice about what circle to share it. This is the box under the ‘Share what’s new’ box. It says ‘Add more people’ with a link that brings up some defaults, such as ‘Public, Extended Circles, etc’.</p>
<p>I send most things to Public, because I’m a writer and want people who aren’t even using Google+ to see many of the questions I write and the work I feature on Google+. Occasionally, when I’m discussing a more sensitive topic, I choose to share with all of my followers. This means that random people can’t see it. I can also start a small gathering with 3 people, if I want to.</p>
<p>In this same box, is where I choose to direct message a single person.</p>
<h4>Streams for two</h4>
<p>First I delete ‘public’ or ‘followers’ until this box is completely blank. </p>
<p>Then I type +[Name] into that box. </p>
<p>Then I can share a message with only one person. This message I’ve sent is invisible to everyone but that person who I’ve directed the message to. I’ve created a direct message, from me to you. </p>
<p>//</p>
<p>Another option is to +[Name] a person and include ‘Public’ in the share box as well. This will start a dialog between that person and yourself on your Google+ stream page. This is a great way to conduct interviews! </p>
<p>Keep in mind that it might be best to ask permission. “Are you willing to have a public dialog with me on my Google+ page around this topic?” This way I have a choice to answer you or not.</p>
<p>//</p>
<p>In order to see who is able to view a message, you can always click on the ‘Limited’ text at the top of the message. If it says ‘Public’, it means everyone can see it.</p>
