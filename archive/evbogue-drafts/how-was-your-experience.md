<p class="date">October 14, 2011</p>
<p itemprop="description"><p><strong>“What do you think?”</strong></p>
<p>I get asked this question a lot.</p>
<p>When I hear “what do you think?” I have a hard time coming up with an answer. In a way, it feels intrusive, because I value my time more than anything. Being prompted to think about something directly that I usually didn’t ask to think about feels like an intrusion to me. </p>
<p><strong>Here’s what I think:</strong> let’s ask a better question. One that doesn’t ask for me to offer an opinion or advice, but instead asks for something that I (and you) can more easily connect with.</p>
<p>I’ve been asking this question a lot lately, and it appears to land well with people.</p>
<p>It’s a simple change of wording: “how was your experience?”</p>
<p>This is an invitation to speak from experience. It’s an invitation to the other person to check in with themselves, and answer with what they see.</p>
<p>If I don’t know how to answer, now I can say: “I don’t know, I haven’t experienced that.” You can too.</p>
<p>This feels kinder, to my email box and yours.</p>
