<p><a href="/assets/posts/how-to-unplug-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/how-to-unplug-img1.jpg" alt="" width="262" height="350"/></a>Kevin Kelly believes that the human race is <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/lang/eng/kevin_kelly_on_the_next_5_000_days_of_the_web.html">building the planet into one giant brain</a> with our technology.</p>
<p>He&#8217;s not sure what it&#8217;s called, but some call it The One, others call it The Cloud.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m not sure what to believe, but I do know one thing: we&#8217;re all plugged in. Some of us never unplug.</p>
<p>This is why I believe that it&#8217;s an absolute imperative to turn it all off once in awhile.</p>
<p>We have to take the time to get away from the cloud, head into the forest, and discover what it is to not be part of <a href="http://scienceblogs.com/insolence/LocutusOfBorg.jpg">The Borg</a>.</p>
<p>I love being plugged in, but there are stories on the fringes of my imagination of a day when people can&#8217;t turn it off. When to tweet is a necessity of human nature.</p>
<p>We are closer to this reality than you and I believe.</p>
<p>Recently one of my heroes, <a href="http://www.gwenbell.com/blog/2010/8/2/slow-steep-a-month-of-drinking-deep.html">Gwen Bell took a month off of the net</a>. No working, no tweeting, no checking email. She&#8217;s back now, but the experience taught her to listen to herself again.</p>
<p>This is why I&#8217;m leaving you for awhile.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll be camping in the wilds of upper Wisconsin from August 10th-23rd.</p>
<p>I won&#8217;t tweet. I won&#8217;t check email. I&#8217;m going without a computer, I&#8217;m going where there is no 3G (for now.)</p>
<p>My blog and business will run on auto-pilot. I have a post scheduled for Tuesday, Monday, and Monday.</p>
<p>Comments will be off until further notice.</p>
<p>If you miss me, read my blog archives. A good place to start is <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/2009/10/page/3/">at the beginning</a>. Or, <a href="http://minimalistbusiness.com/">check out my books</a>. Some say they&#8217;re quite good.</p>
<p>Some say that I will miss opportunities while I&#8217;m gone. I think going away is the opportunity.</p>
<p>August is a slow month on the Internet. Many people take this time to get reacquainted with themselves. Maybe this is a good time for you to do that as well.</p>
<p>We&#8217;ll all be back in September, and the work will continue.</p>
<p>For now, a break.</p>
