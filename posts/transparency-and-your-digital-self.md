<p><em>January 8th 2011, Brooklyn NY, The School House.</em></p>
<p><a href="http://instagr.am/p/6x4x/"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/transparency-and-your-digital-self-img1.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300"/></a>I&#8217;ve been coming back to the idea of the digital self over and over recently, in particular how we portray ourselves online.</p>
<p>Over the last few months I&#8217;ve had the pleasure of meeting dozens of successful bloggers in person, and I&#8217;ve always been surprised at just how similar they are to their digital selves &#8212; the them that exists online in the cloud, the one that you see when you check their blogs, Twitter, etc. A digital self is self-perpetuating and lives independently of your own physical body.</p>
<p>Perhaps transparency and the success of a digital self go hand-in-hand? If you&#8217;re able to communicate your true nature online, you will be able to <strong>reach deeper into everyone else&#8217;s souls across the ether</strong> in order to stir up some real feelings.</p>
<p>We can tell if you&#8217;re holding something back from us. I don&#8217;t think everyone can, but we can. The ones of us who were raised by the Internet. Neck-deep in the cybersystem every night searching for the next answer, or letting the next answer search for us.</p>
<p>This is why I always find the time <a href="http://lexgarey.com/">Lex Garey&#8217;s work</a>. When she writes a post, I know it&#8217;s coming from her and not some artificial sense of what she wants you to think she is.</p>
<p>Being on the <strong>Internet gives you an opportunity to shape-shift</strong> into anyone you want. Maybe you&#8217;ll choose a cute little humanoid-kitten avatar, instead of your true face. However, this choice comes at a cost.</p>
<p>When you choose to hide your true nature, whether you&#8217;re going to some artificial extreme or you&#8217;re simply pretending to be part of the status quo, <strong>we sense the disconnect</strong>.</p>
<p>We&#8217;re still humans using the Internet, and we use it to connect with other humans. We can read between lines. We&#8217;re not going to feel the same way about your WoW Avatar as we do about your beautiful human face.</p>
<p>We think we have a choice about how much we share, and we do. Life is lived in high-bandwidth, but <strong>we make decisions about everything we funnel</strong> out into the net (for now, that will change when the bandwidth gets wide and free enough for complete life-casting.)</p>
<p>So, I can choose to show you a picture of my abs five minutes after I woke up this morning. Or, I could have decided to show you a picture of my shoes.</p>
<p>Which do you connect with most? Which builds a more compelling story of who I am to you?</p>
<p>..and more importantly, how can you bring more of your true nature out onto the Net? It might just be what the world needs.</p>
