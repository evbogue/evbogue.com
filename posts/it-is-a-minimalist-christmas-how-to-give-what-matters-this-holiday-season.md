<p><em>Uh oh</em>, it&#8217;s almost Black Friday.</p>
<p>Hopefully the idea of running out to consume, after you&#8217;ve consumed all of that turkey, gives the shudders. It certainly gives them to me. The idea of people cramming half-off things into shopping cards actually makes me want to lock myself in the cellar and reemerge after the holidays are over.</p>
<p>Nothing is sadder than running around a store frantically trying to buy useless things at a discount, then pawning them off on some poor friend or family member who has to deal with that object until they can safely throw it away without you noticing.</p>
<p><strong>Stop buying stupid stuff, especially for other people. Please!</strong></p>
<p>The whole idea behind Christmas, or whatever holiday you celebrate is giving right? Let&#8217;s take a moment to think about what giving actually means.</p>
<p>You can give by:</p>
<ul>
<li>Helping someone live a better life.</li>
<li>Making someone smile.</li>
<li>Helping someone achieve something.</li>
<li>Making someone feel good.</li>
<li>Giving someone an opportunity that they would otherwise have.</li>
</ul>
<p>Nowhere that definition can I see &#8216;giving is another useless plastic object they can put in the closet.&#8217;</p>
<p>Most of us have everything that we will ever need already. I only have 97 things, and I still think that&#8217;s way too much. I don&#8217;t need anything else.</p>
<p>To be honest, if I received anything that I couldn&#8217;t immediately benefit me every single day of the year, I&#8217;d ask that the person who gave it to me to return it.</p>
<p><strong>I challenge you to give a gift that<em> actually means something</em> to every person you know this Christmas. </strong></p>
<p>Five minimalist Christmas gifts:</p>
<ol>
<li><strong>Dinner.</strong> I love cooking for people, this is a great way to show to someone that you appreciate them. One way to do this is to give someone a small card inviting them to dinner. If you&#8217;re a terrible cook, take them out for dinner.</li>
<li><strong>Wine.</strong> You can share it and it makes you tipsy. Not Yellowtail or Two Buck Chuck. Good wine, the kind that comes in a bottle and costs a little more than average. A good place to start is a bottle that you&#8217;ve personally enjoyed.</li>
<li><strong>Knowledge.</strong> Buy someone a book that they may read, but MAKE SURE it&#8217;s a good one. There are a lot of crappy books out there, and no one wants a book from the sale rack. Stick with the bestseller lists, or books that you&#8217;ve personally learned a great deal from. If this confuses you, a gift card to a bookstore will do.</li>
<li><strong>Money.</strong> It&#8217;s usable for a lot of things, such as paying down student loan debt or buying groceries.</li>
<li><strong>Opportunities.</strong> Get someone an interview somewhere. Give them a recommendation. Expose a person to the right environment or ideas.</li>
</ol>
<p>Think about it, and look at that list again. You want all of those things, don&#8217;t you? Maybe not the Wine, but you can supplement that with a fine beverage that suits you or chocolate.</p>
<p>If you give plastic crap you will get plastic crap.</p>
<p>If you give what you want, you will get what you want.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s really that simple.</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
