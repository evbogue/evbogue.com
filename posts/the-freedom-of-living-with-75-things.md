<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4370697234/sizes/m/"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/the-freedom-of-living-with-75-things-img1.jpg" alt="" width="263" height="350"/></a>It&#8217;s been awhile since <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?p=429">I counted how many objects</a> I own, so I was surprised when I got everything out today.</p>
<p>I only have 75 things.</p>
<p>This is excellent, I was pretty sure Christmas had run me over 100, but  apparently I&#8217;ve cultivated a natural habit of reduction. For every  object I acquire, I lose two.</p>
<p>If you think 75 things seems like not a lot of stuff to have, <a href="http://mnmlist.com/50-things/">Leo Babauta is now living with 50  things</a>. I&#8217;m envious.</p>
<p><strong>Where my stuff went.</strong></p>
<p>I probably got rid of 15 things when I left Portland for Chicago. I gave away some stuff in Chicago to my siblings. When I moved out of my old apartment in Brooklyn to the one I&#8217;m staying in now, I also gave away a bunch of stuff that I realized I didn&#8217;t need.</p>
<p>Giving away my stuff is very liberating.</p>
<p>The reason I gave everything a count today was because I&#8217;m guest starring on my friend Rachel Solomon&#8217;s personal finance show on <a href="http://plentii.com/">Plentii</a>, Always: Money (first stop Plentii, next stop &#8230;Oprah?) They asked me to bring all of my stuff into Rachel&#8217;s home studio today at 1:30 (which, granted is only 10 blocks away, or I wouldn&#8217;t have agreed to haul my stuff.) I&#8217;ll be sure to let you know when the segment goes live. I&#8217;m really honored to have the opportunity to do this video, as I&#8217;ll be able to share with more people how to live with less.</p>
<p>When you have 75 things, you can move your home in fifteen minutes. Isn&#8217;t that cool?</p>
<p><strong>The shape of things to come.</strong></p>
<p>This is also good to know, because I&#8217;m moving again soon. My girlfriend and I are taking off to San Francisco Bay on May 15th, where we&#8217;ll probably get an apartment in Oakland or Berkeley (leaning towards Oakland.) The original plan was to move to Seattle, but after a lot of thought and discussion, we decided that SF was more our style right now. Of course, if we end up not liking it, we&#8217;ll just move somewhere else!</p>
<p>Because California is warmer, I anticipate being about to get rid of many of these winter-specific items in my wardrobe. I can&#8217;t wait to say goodbye to winter.</p>
<p>I should be able to cut down my possessions to less than 50 things before we relocate to San Francisco in May.</p>
<p><strong>My 75 things.</strong></p>
<p>Please note, I do not count books. As you may already know, <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?page_id=966">I read a book a week</a>. I have around 15 books at the moment, which I try to get rid of as soon as I read them.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m also not counting shared items such as cooking stuff, furniture, etc. We don&#8217;t really have much shared stuff, and we&#8217;re going to sell most of it before the move.</p>
<p>I also don&#8217;t count electrical cords and USB cables as things. I kind of wish technology <a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/lang/eng/eric_giler_demos_wireless_electricity.html">would power itself and connect wirelessly</a> actually, because cords are annoying.</p>
<p>I also still have my old Macbook that died two weeks ago (I had to buy a new one.) I&#8217;m going to try and sell it for spare parts next week, or recycle it.</p>

<p><strong>How did I start living with 75 things?</strong></p>
<p>I get a lot of emails from people saying &#8216;wow, how did you start living with so little?&#8217; The answer is simple: I wanted freedom. I want to be able to move whenever I feel the need. I wanted to quit my day job and work for myself. I did this in August of last year, when I jumped on a plane to Portland. I discuss a lot of the details earlier in this blog&#8217;s history, and also in <a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/"><em>The Art of Being Minimalist</em></a>.</p>
<p>There are so many advantages of restricting yourself to less than 100 things.</p>
<ul>
<li>Freedom to move whenever you want.</li>
<li>You can opt out of the endless cycle of consumerism.</li>
<li>People give you wine as gifts, instead of stupid plastic thingies.</li>
<li>You have less impact on the planet.</li>
<li>You can focus on the important, instead of doing housework.</li>
</ul>
<p>There are obviously many more reasons why it&#8217;s a great idea to live with less. Can you think of any good reasons? Leave them in the comments!</p>
