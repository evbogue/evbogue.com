<p>The most important strategy that a minimalist businessperson can employ is simply helping people achieve their goals.</p>
<p>We all subsist on valuable information, and yet it&#8217;s so difficult to find in this world. There&#8217;s so much fluff, and McDonald&#8217;s-chicken-nugget-type info that&#8217;s meant to be consumed but not used to better yourself.</p>
<p>When you make the conscious decision to become a filter for other people&#8217;s reality, in order to cut out all of the crap that doesn&#8217;t matter, you can support yourself by supporting work that matters.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s the not so shocking truth about how I made an additional $2,300 in one day for my minimalist business last month: I simply dropped an affiliate link to Chris Guillebeau&#8217;s <a href="http://unconventionalguides.com/cmd.php?Clk=3720851">Empire Builder Kit</a> in the bottom of my blog post on the day that it relaunched.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s the exact text I used:</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;If you’re interested. My friend Chris Guillebeau is re-launching his <a href="http://unconventionalguides.com/cmd.php?Clk=3720851">Empire Builder Kit</a> for a second time today (May 18th 2010 from 10am EST until May 19th at 10am EST) for 24 hours only.</p>
<p>The premise is simple: case studies including actual monetary figures by people running very small businesses who make tons of cash a year. In addition to that, you receive one email a day (that’s 365 tips!) that will help you build a business destined for world domination in at least one year.&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>As you can see, it was nothing much. The quality of Chris&#8217;s work speaks for itself.</p>
<p>From what I&#8217;ve heard from colleagues, a number of other bloggers with relatively small followings (in the 1000-3000 subscriber range) were able to pull commissions in the quad-digits as well.</p>
<p><strong>Why Empire Builder?</strong></p>
<p>The case seems obvious, to me. The guide is jam-packed with information on how to create what Chris calls an Empire &#8212; essentially a very small business based around the work that you&#8217;re passionate about.</p>
<p>Add on top of this an entire year worth of content that&#8217;s pumped to your inbox daily, and you can see that the investment goes way above and beyond the actual price you&#8217;re paying for it.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t really need to say anymore about Chris&#8217;s work, you get the idea how valuable this is.</p>
<p>Anyone who purchased <a href="http://unconventionalguides.com/cmd.php?Clk=3720851">Empire Builder</a> should also be affiliating for it (you can join <a href="http://unconventionalguides.com/affiliates.htm">Chris&#8217;s affiliate program here</a>,) why not put the word out there and pay back your purchase investment with two sales? This is the magic of digital distribution.</p>
<p>Note: The Empire Builder Kit isn&#8217;t available right now, but will be relaunched again next week [<strong>UPDATE: Empire Builder is now available for the foreseeable future.</strong>]</p>
<p><strong>Why supporting quality work can support you.</strong></p>
<p>The reason I was able to pull such a large figure on one product in one day is simple:</p>
<p><strong>1. Build trust. </strong>The reason that I&#8217;m able to pull big numbers like this is because I&#8217;ve build trust with my supporters. They know I&#8217;m not going to throw them expensive garbage, and if I did that would burn away my support. Share only the work that creates value for your readers, and they will support you.</p>
<p><strong>2. Show the benefits.</strong> Don&#8217;t tell, show your readers how the investment paid off for you. What did you learn? What surprised you? What completely blew your mind? Don&#8217;t sell crap that doesn&#8217;t blow your mind.</p>
<p><strong>3. Make it clear that this isn&#8217;t for everyone.</strong> Not everyone is going to support you with money. Not everyone is supporting you will have the same interests or needs as everyone else. Some people need one product that helps them, others will need another. Don&#8217;t force things down people&#8217;s throats, simply suggest they check it out and purchase it if they think it will help them.</p>
