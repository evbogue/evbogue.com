<p><a href="/assets/posts/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/minimalist-business-how-to-live-and-work-anywhere-img1.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="250"/></a><strong>A brief history of being minimalist.</strong></p>
<p>In September of last year I quit my job, and hopped on a plane to Portland Oregon in search of freedom. In order to survive, I had to make a choice that many people are having to make in this economy:</p>
<p>I had to embrace minimalism in order to pursue what was important to me.</p>
<p>I started living with less than 100 things, biked and walked everywhere, survived on less than $3,000 for three months, and practiced time management techniques to spend less time doing work and more time making work that matters.</p>
<p>In February of this year I launched <a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/"><em>The Art of Being Minimalist</em></a>, a little e-book with a powerful message: what would you be able to accomplish if you lived with less?</p>
<p>What really surprised me, is that a little e-book about being minimalist could completely support my lifestyle. I could move anywhere (and I did, traveling from Portland to Chicago to New York and then relocating to San Francisco last month at limited expense.) I also didn&#8217;t need to have a day job, which was the most important element for me.</p>
<p><strong>These reasons form the basis for the work I&#8217;ve put into Minimalist Business</strong><em><strong>:</strong></em></p>
<ol>
<li>Your business doesn&#8217;t need to cost as much as you think.</li>
<li>If you opt-out of physical media and avoid gatekeepers, you can keep 50-100% of your profits.</li>
<li>If choose to automate your business, you can create passive income, which means you don&#8217;t have to work so much anymore either.</li>
</ol>
<p>The number one reason for creating <em>Minimalist Business</em> is to help you create one too.</p>
<p>When I started writing about the success I was having with my minimalist business, I began receiving a flood of emails asking me how I was able to do it. The problem with answering emails is that it only helps one person, and the strategy isn&#8217;t scalable.</p>
<p>I hope <em>Minimalist Business</em> answers any questions you have about creating a zero-overhead business to support your minimalist lifestyle anywhere in the world.</p>
<p><strong>Why create a minimalist business?</strong></p>
<p>We live in interesting times. The economy still hasn&#8217;t recovered from the greatest recession since the great depression. This means that there aren&#8217;t a lot of fulfilling job opportunities out there anymore.</p>
<p>People (<a href="http://artofgreatthings.com/2010/06/leaving-my-job-looking-forward/">like Jeffrey F. Tang</a>) are waking up and realizing that in order to create a fulfilling job, they have to design that life for themselves.</p>
<p>We have to change the way we create businesses, and how we do important work, if we are going to design lives that are worth living.</p>
<p>Job security in the modern economy is a myth that we&#8217;ve been taught to accept by corporations who are forced to only care about the bottom line because of endless bureaucracy. People are beginning to realize that the best job security is the work you create to support yourself.</p>
<p>A minimalist business can help you achieve what <a href="http://www.chrisbrogan.com/escape-velocity/">Chris Brogan likes to call &#8220;escape velocity&#8221;</a> and enable you to build recurring income outside of your day job in order to free yourself.</p>
<p>Or you can just jump head-first like I did, live with less, and do the work that matters.</p>
<p><strong>Why Minimalist Business isn&#8217;t for everyone.</strong></p>
<p>This work isn&#8217;t meant for everyone. It takes hard work, dedication, and most important, the will power to opt-out of assumed systems and methods for doing business.</p>
<p>No one is going to force you to reign in your spending, reduce your business overhead to zero, or stop checking your email 35 times a day in order to do work that matters.</p>
<p>Some people are better off with 9-5 day jobs. In a lot of ways they&#8217;re much easier (though definitely not safer.) Some people like living in the same city, commuting to the same job every day. You can just sit there and do what you&#8217;re told, for most people that&#8217;s a perfectly acceptable way to live until they retire. If you&#8217;re one of these people, <em>Minimalist Business</em> isn&#8217;t really meant for you.</p>
