<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4226784090/sizes/m/"><img class="alignleft" title="Flying over Brooklyn" src="/assets/posts/how-i-am-making-6-changes-in-2010-img1.jpg" alt="" width="270" height="360"/></a>Have you seen Leo Babauta&#8217;s new project, <a href="http://www.6changes.com/">6 Changes</a>?</p>
<p>6 Changes is a brilliant no-nonsense antidote to the often failed new years resolution.</p>
<p>On the blog, Leo lays out in his signature uncomplicated prose his new simple philosophy towards habit change. Pick 6 habits you want to incorporate to your life in the next year, then dedicate 8 weeks to building each habit. This gives you roughly 2 months to develop each habit in a year, and by the end of the year you&#8217;ve made 6 changes in your life.</p>
<p>Well, it&#8217;s no secret that I&#8217;m a <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?p=569">big fan of Leo&#8217;s work</a>. His approach to life often aligns perfectly with my own.</p>
<p>For those who aren&#8217;t familiar with Leo&#8217;s other blog, <a href="http://www.zenhabits.net/">Zen Habits</a>, I suggest you check it out. It&#8217;s a brilliant blog on how to simplify your life and achieve your goals.</p>

<p>If you haven&#8217;t checked out Leo&#8217;s book on <a href="http://zenhabits.net/2009/09/my-new-ebook-the-simple-guide-to-a-minimalist-life/">being minimalist</a>, I highly recommend it.</p>
<p><strong>I&#8217;m taking 6 Changes for a test drive after New Years.</strong></p>
<p>I have some very important habits I need to adopt for next year to be successful, and I hope Leo&#8217;s philosophy for change can help achieve my goals.</p>
<p>Maybe you&#8217;re interested in joining me?</p>
<p><strong>The six changes I&#8217;m going to make in 2010.</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Wake up at 6am in the morning daily</li>
<li>Eat locally grown food</li>
<li>Move to Seattle</li>
<li>Pay off my student loans</li>
<li>Train to be a yoga teacher</li>
<li>Cultivate this blog until it&#8217;s primary source of income</li>
</ul>
<p>Now I realize some of these might seem like big changes to expect to achieve in a year, but as Leo suggests in his blog, it&#8217;s important not to focus on the end point. I&#8217;ll focus on the small details, and just focus on one habit for two months at a time.</p>
<p>I did some pretty crazy things in 2009, so I expect with a little help from Leo&#8217;s 6 Changes philosophy, I can hope to achieve my 2010 goals as well.</p>
<p><strong>Last year I adopted these habits:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Reduced my possessions to 100 things</li>
<li>Quit my day job</li>
<li>Adopted a location independent lifestyle</li>
<li>Began doing yoga most days of the week</li>
<li>Started Far Beyond The Stars and began publishing articles three days a week</li>
</ul>
<p>None of that was easy, but I realized that if I took simple steps towards achieving those goals, I could realistically expect to complete the goals in a year.</p>
<p>You can make these changes too, but first you have to make the decision to change your life.</p>
<p><strong>My first change for 2010: becoming an early riser.</strong></p>
<p>I&#8217;ve decide to build a habit of waking up early for my first big change. I&#8217;ve wanted to start waking up before the sun comes up for a long time. I&#8217;m one of those people who enjoys sitting in the comfort of bed for a long morning.</p>
<p>I have a lot of goals to achieve next year, not to mention the fact that I&#8217;m self-employed now.</p>
<p>The time has come to kick the extended bed habit and get up earlier.</p>
<p>I believe that I&#8217;d really enjoy the extra time in the morning to do yoga and then focus on creating incredibly useful content for Far Beyond The Stars. Having a healthy work</p>
<p><strong>Build </strong><a href="http://6changes.com/post/296037328/anticipation"><strong>anticipation</strong></a><strong> for the change.</strong></p>
<p>According to Leo&#8217;s 6 Changes philosophy, I&#8217;m not allowed to start exactly on the 1st of January. Start on New Years is just too dramatic, plus I&#8217;ll probably have a hangover from celebrating with my family.</p>
<p>In order to set a realistic agenda for healthy habit development, I have to anticipate the change.</p>
<p>So I intend to start working on developing the habit of early rising on January 7th 2010.</p>
<p>This is the first day I&#8217;m back in New York, and the change in scenery always help me with changes in habit, so I anticipate a good start.</p>
<p><strong>The trigger for the first of my 6 Changes.</strong></p>
<p>Leo&#8217;s 6 Changes philosophy <a href="http://6changes.com/post/288258857/triggers">suggests using a trigger</a> to help you form a healthy habit (or kick a bad one.)</p>
<p>For waking up early, the trigger is obviously the alarm. However, relying on an alarm could lead to failure, so I have to create a trigger.</p>
<p>For my trigger, I&#8217;ll be placing my iPhone (which is my alarm clock) on my yoga mat in our living room in Brooklyn. I&#8217;m going to simply get out of bed, turn off the alarm, and sit myself in child&#8217;s pose on the yoga mat.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ll begin to breathe, gradually wake up, and then begin practicing yoga.</p>
<p>I already do yoga almost every day, which I enjoy very much, so having this as my trigger just seems natural. Simple, achievable, enjoyable.</p>
<p><strong>Set small achievable goals.</strong></p>
<p>I eventually want to wake up at 6am every day, but right now wake up schedule is not a schedule at all. Some mornings I wake up at 6am, others I wake up at 11am. There is no system, there is no regularity.</p>
<p>I need to change this to a regular system, so I can start to develop a healthy creative habit in order to focus on my work and creating awesome content for this blog.</p>
<p>I have two months to develop this habit, so I want to start with realistic expectations for myself.</p>
<p>Leo suggests breaking your habit down into <strong>8 ridiculously easy steps</strong>, and the easiest way for me to do that is to gradually work my way down to 6am by 15 minutes.</p>
<p>Here is my plan to gradually work up to waking up at my desired time.</p>
<ul>
<li>Week 1: 7:45am</li>
<li>Week 2: 7:30am</li>
<li>Week 3: 7:15am</li>
<li>Week 4: 7:00am</li>
<li>Week 5: 7:45am</li>
<li>Week 6: 6:30am</li>
<li>Week 7: 6:15am</li>
<li>Week 8: 6:00am</li>
</ul>
<p>Once I hit 6:00am I&#8217;ll have established a habit of waking up early, and it won&#8217;t be hard to get out of bed.</p>
<p><strong>You must have accountability</strong></p>
<p>One of the most important elements of Leo&#8217;s 6 Changes philosophy is <a href="http://6changes.com/post/284561373/accountability">accountability</a>. In order to develop a habit, there has to be peer pressure involved.</p>
<p>Often I rely on self-motivation, which is sometimes successful.</p>
<p>Waking up early is a habit I&#8217;ve tried to form at least 7 of times without succeeding, so I need you to help me.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m going to be posting my daily wake up time on <a href="http://daytum.com/evbogue">a Daytum</a> that I&#8217;ve set up. As you can see, right now my wake up time is basically schizophrenic. Once January 7th comes around, I&#8217;m going to start committing to the wake up times that I&#8217;ve described above.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re interested, feel free to follow along as I progress. If I&#8217;m not doing well (which I doubt, you&#8217;ll all be watching me.) drop me a line on <a href="http://www.twitter.com/evbogue">Twitter</a> and tell me to get back on track.</p>
