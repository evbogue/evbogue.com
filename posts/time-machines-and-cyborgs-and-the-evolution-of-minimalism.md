<p><em> </em></p>
<p><em>January 21st 2011. Boulder, CO. </em></p>
<p><a href="/assets/posts/time-machines-and-cyborgs-and-the-evolution-of-minimalism-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/time-machines-and-cyborgs-and-the-evolution-of-minimalism-img1.jpg" alt="" width="300" height="300"/></a>I love Mars Dorian, and I laughed when he said <a href="http://www.marsdorian.com/2011/01/digital-persona/">&#8220;What kind of pills did you slip into your muesli?&#8221;</a> the other day when we were Skyping. While Mars and I aren&#8217;t exactly <a href="http://janstewart.com.au/ibc">#ibcing</a> yet, after the in-person Skype chat we were way closer to being on the same page.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s true, Far Beyond The Stars lately has been kind of well, far beyond the stars.</p>
<p>The content here has been what we meditators like to call delving deep. It&#8217;s a brief glance into the future which is now for a few of us &#8212; I&#8217;m a timelord, you&#8217;re stepping into my Tardis when you&#8217;re with me in this space (it&#8217;s <a href="http://letter.ly/evbogue">bigger on the inside</a> than it is on the out.)</p>
<p>I&#8217;m hoping that the content that I still post on this blog will bring you to a page where you can delve deeper into the work I&#8217;m doing using the <a href="http://letter.ly/evbogue">Letter.ly</a> or the new book I&#8217;m working on.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.gwenbell.com/">Gwen</a> and I have been talking a lot lately about how we wish the Internet was more 4-dimensional. It needs the intelligence to spot a person who&#8217;s at the point in their lives where they really need a 5-ways to declutter your desk post and give it to them, instead of a deep philosophical piece on assimilating other cyborgs into the collective.</p>
<p>The truth is that my work moves, it changes, it evolves. Your work does and will too.</p>
<p>Unfortunately I can&#8217;t write simple posts about ultra-light travel anymore. If you want a great collection of those, <a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/">The Art of Being Minimalist</a> really sums up how to go minimalist from the human perspective (though cyborg eyes will glaze over at this point when they read the content that I wrote a year ago.)</p>
<p>My physical/mental/spiritual body has been evolving, it&#8217;s tapped into the exponential nature of technology, and so my cultural evolution is tethered to the rate of computer chip evolution. This evolution is quickly closing on something like a million to the power of a million times better than it was every year, just wait until we get exponentially accelerating quantum computer chips. How will you possibly understand me then if you aren&#8217;t on quantum-<a href="http://www.twitter.com/evbogue">Twitter</a>?</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
<p>Minimalism was assimilated by the cyborgs.</p>
<p>This didn&#8217;t happen because cyborgs wanted to become minimalists, it&#8217;s because we the cyborgs didn&#8217;t know who we were when we became minimalists.</p>
<p>For eons humans only made physical tools, then all of a sudden we began to develop mental tools. This much is clear from <a href="www.ted.com/talks/amber_case_we_are_all_cyborgs_now.html">Amber Case&#8217;s brilliant Ted Talk</a> that you all should have watched by now.</p>
<p>Cyborgs are simply humans who made the shift from physical tools to mental tools. Eventually there will be enough cyborgs that we can call them humans again, and we&#8217;ll call people who still use physical tools something else.</p>
<p>I don&#8217;t want you to be called something else, so that&#8217;s why I keep telling you to get on <a href="http://www.twitter.com/evbogue">Twitter</a>.</p>
<p>When you develop mental tools, in a very short time you begin to realize that you don&#8217;t need your physical possessions anymore, so you kind of just let them go &#8212; it doesn&#8217;t hurt anymore. I saw this happen within a few weeks to <a href="http://www.escapingthe9to5.com/">Maren Kate</a> after she went location independent when <a href="http://zirtual.com/">Zirtual</a> was accepted into an incubator program in Palo Alto. She instantly shifted from physical tools to mental tools. Welcome to cyborgia Maren. <img src="/assets/posts/time-machines-and-cyborgs-and-the-evolution-of-minimalism-img2.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"/> </p>
<p>This doesn&#8217;t mean that minimalism never existed, just that there is much more depth under the idea that many of us previously imagined. I predict that a lot of minimalist websites in the next year (actually, probably a lot faster than that) will fold or move on to deeper topics as our own evolution progresses towards the future.</p>
<p>If you&#8217;re a minimalist blogger, it might be wise to quickly snag a URL that isn&#8217;t &#8216;minimalist-X&#8217; before everyone evolves exponentially past what you&#8217;re writing about. However, be mindful of what your readers need. Mine need cyborgs right now, your readers might be in a different place than mine are.</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
<p>As always, the deeper stuff is going to go on the <a href="http://letter.ly/evbogue">Letter.ly</a>. The blog is the surface, you have to commit to stepping into the time-machine.</p>
<p>Required reading on <a href="http://janstewart.com.au/ibc">how cyborgs are using intuitive back channel #ibc by Jan Stewart</a>.</p>
