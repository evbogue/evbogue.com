<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4269823169/sizes/m/"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-878" title="empirestate" src="/assets/posts/46-ways-to-let-go-of-stuff-and-other-things-img1.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="375"/></a>It&#8217;s incredibly hard to let go. I know so many people who have so much trouble with letting go that they have houses full of stuff they haven&#8217;t used in ten years. They&#8217;ve stayed in a job they don&#8217;t enjoy, just because. Or they keep crying over relationships that ended months ago.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s difficult to say goodbye.</p>
<p>This article is mostly about stuff, but this can apply to people and places and jobs that left us a tiny bit scarred.</p>
<p>It takes courage to stop dwelling and move to new terrain.</p>
<p>Forty five things in a box in a closet you haven&#8217;t touched in five years is one thing you might need to let go. Hanging out with those friends who always get you into trouble is another. Driving a car just because you always have can be something you can let go as well. Maybe you need to let go a snack food.</p>
<p>It takes courage to give something up, I understand that. Trust me, I&#8217;ve had plenty of my own personal battles with letting go of people, places and things. I imagine most people have.</p>
<p>One of the traits that most successful people share is the ability to know when to move on.</p>
<p>Because this is a problem that all of us face, I&#8217;ve taken time to prepare a list of 46 ways to let go of the old. I&#8217;m sure I left something out. I&#8217;m sure most of these won&#8217;t work for everyone. However, maybe one or two of these will stick and help you let go of something.</p>
<p>&#8230;this way you can make room for the new.</p>
<p><strong>46 ways you can let go.</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Throw it away.</li>
<li>Give it away.</li>
<li>Recycle it.</li>
<li>Tell it to go home.</li>
<li>Make a list of things you&#8217;d rather have, or be doing.</li>
<li>Don&#8217;t let yourself see it for a month.</li>
<li>Don&#8217;t keep anything you don&#8217;t use or see at least once a month.</li>
<li>Make a diagram of everywhere you spend your time.</li>
<li>Eliminate time suckers.</li>
<li>Trade one thing or activity for another that you like better.</li>
<li>Throw it into the ocean (if it won&#8217;t hurt the fish).</li>
<li>Break it.</li>
<li>Tell someone to take it away.</li>
<li>Sell it to someone who needs it.</li>
<li>Turn it into artwork.</li>
<li>Display it at an art gallery.</li>
<li>Get a second opinion.</li>
<li>Limit yourself to 100 things.</li>
<li>Burn it on your roof or backyard.</li>
<li>Tell it you love it, but you can&#8217;t be together.</li>
<li>It&#8217;s you, it&#8217;s me.</li>
<li>Throw a party and tell everyone to take something home with them.</li>
<li>Bury it in the backyard so an archeologist can dig it up in 2000 years and think it&#8217;s more important than it actually is.</li>
<li>Give it to your mom.</li>
<li>Reinvent it as something new and interesting.</li>
<li><a href="mailto:Evbogue@gmail.com">Email</a> a picture of it to me and ask if it&#8217;s worth keeping (my answer is usually no.)</li>
<li>Acknowledge that it&#8217;s hurting you.</li>
<li>Think about what ultimate freedom means to you.</li>
<li>Contemplate your minimalist destination.</li>
<li>Give it to someone who has nothing.</li>
<li>Put it in an outbox.</li>
<li>Bury it on the side of a mountain and tell no one.</li>
<li>Leave it on the subway.</li>
<li>Take a picture of it.</li>
<li>Post that picture on craigslist.</li>
<li>Drop it off the side of a building at night, and then clean it up first thing in the morning.</li>
<li>Destroy it and hang it from the ceiling.</li>
<li>Mail to to a random person in the phone book.</li>
<li>Sell it and donate the money to a cause that you believe in.</li>
<li>Leave it in a box on your doorstep, with a sign that says &#8216;free&#8217;.</li>
<li>Take a vacation from it all.</li>
<li>Stay at a friend&#8217;s house for a week.</li>
<li>Take it to your recycling center free box.</li>
<li>Tweet #minimalist and ask if you should keep it.</li>
<li>Move somewhere you&#8217;ve always wanted to live.</li>
<li>Take only a backpack.</li>
</ol>
<p>What do you need to let go? Let me and many remarkable readers of Far Beyond The Stars know in the comments!</p>
