<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4331205326/"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/7-simple-ways-you-can-disconnect-img1.jpg" alt="" width="263" height="350"/></a>In the modern age we think we have to constantly rush from this to that. We think we have to wake up and work every morning. We think we have to constantly respond to e-mails.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ve had so many interview requests to respond to about <a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/">The Art of Being Minimalist</a>, it&#8217;s humbling to know that so many people care, but overwhelming. I love doing interviews, and it&#8217;s exciting to have so much interest. But I&#8217;m tired!</p>
<p>I imagine your work week could have been equally as exciting and busy as mine was this last week.</p>
<p>That&#8217;s why I&#8217;m going to take some serious disconnect time this weekend. I&#8217;m going to pull the plug, so to speak.</p>
<p>I hope that you&#8217;ll join me.</p>
<p><strong>Here&#8217;s a couple of great ways to disconnect:</strong></p>
<p><strong>1, Take a social network hiatus.</strong><br/>
Don&#8217;t Tweet, Facebook, or engage with people on any social networking platform that you may use. It&#8217;s really fun to Tweet and hear all of the amazing people respond. Do you really want to spend all Sunday morning glued to Tweetdeck though? Cook someone a good breakfast instead!</p>
<p><strong>2, Ignore all calls. </strong></p>
<p>Don&#8217;t answer the phone at all this weekend. It doesn&#8217;t matter if it&#8217;s your mom or your boss calling, just choose not to pick up the telephone. In fact, turn it off. Go to the beach instead, if you live somewhere that&#8217;s not as cold as it is here in New York. Otherwise, maybe just grab a coffee and watch people walk by.</p>
<p><strong>3, Don&#8217;t check your email.</strong></p>
<p>Just let it sit there, trust me, it will be there come Monday morning. Too often we spend hours of our lives hitting the refresh button on e-mail. Take the exact opposite approach and don&#8217;t check it at all.</p>
<p><strong>4, Spend a day in silence. </strong></p>
<p>Just go about your day without speaking to anyone. Observe your thoughts. Read a book. Be sure to let anyone who might be offended know what you&#8217;re doing, so they don&#8217;t get mad.</p>
<p><strong>5, Refuse to buy anything. </strong></p>
<p>Take a break from consumerism and don&#8217;t go shopping or eat out for one day. Make sure you have enough food to prepare before you start this. Leave your credit cards and cash at home if you go out.</p>
<p><strong>6, Don&#8217;t use any electricity. </strong></p>
<p>Unplug all of the appliances and lights in your house (don&#8217;t do this to the fridge, your food will spoil.) Pretend you&#8217;re no-impact man for a day, and see how it is. You&#8217;ll notice that you won&#8217;t have any light after a certain hour, so either light a candle or sit in silence until it&#8217;s time for bed.</p>
<p><strong>7, Don&#8217;t use transportation. </strong></p>
<p>Don&#8217;t take the subway, don&#8217;t drive your car, don&#8217;t even bike. Just walk if you need to go anywhere. I love walking into Manhattan on weekends to take Yoga. It makes me appreciate my surroundings so much more. A two hour walk somewere can be very meditative.</p>
<p>How do you disconnect?</p>
