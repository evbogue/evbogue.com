<p>I&#8217;ve been doing Yoga daily since I arrived in Portland, almost to the point of having my practice be the focus of my day, and it&#8217;s had some interesting effects:</p>
<p>1, My practice has accelerated.<br/>
I&#8217;m starting to feel like my practice has begun to develop. I&#8217;m getting deeper into poses, and with that comes a sense of effortlessness, which is really important.</p>
<p>2, I&#8217;m becoming more relaxed.<br/>
There&#8217;s a lot going on in my life right now: I&#8217;m freelancing full-time, which has its challenges, I just moved to a new city, I&#8217;m separated from my awesome girlfriend, ultimately I have no idea what I&#8217;m doing (like the rest of us, I suppose.) By practicing Yoga daily I&#8217;ve found that I can maintain peace and calm amidst the chaos.</p>
<p>3: I&#8217;m concentrated in the moment.<br/>
A lot of people practice yoga like it&#8217;s a race. They&#8217;re racing to the end of each pose, and eventually each class, and then they race out of class to the shower. I&#8217;m coming to realize (more so than I have in the past) just how important it is to move through every moment, in yoga and in life, as if it&#8217;s the only moment. Yoga is about the journey, not how fast you can get to the end of the pose.</p>
<p>The most powerful changes come in the in-between moments. If you&#8217;re not paying attention you&#8217;ll miss them.</p>
