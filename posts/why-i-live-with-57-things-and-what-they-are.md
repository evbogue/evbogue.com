<p><a href="/assets/posts/why-i-live-with-57-things-and-what-they-are-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/why-i-live-with-57-things-and-what-they-are-img1.jpg" alt="" width="262" height="350"/></a>Long time readers of this blog, and anyone who&#8217;s picked up a copy of <em><a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/art-of-being-minimalist.php">The Art of Being Minimalist</a></em> know that I&#8217;ve had a storied relationship with the personal possessions that I own and acquire.</p>
<p><strong>Why you have so much stuff.</strong></p>
<p>Basically, I think that we&#8217;ve been duped into buying things by an advertisement-dominated society for the last 50 years. The Internet is just now allowing us to break free of this mess and start to realize that the junk doesn&#8217;t matter.</p>
<p>Because I live with less stuff, and don&#8217;t buy much, all of the money I make from my minimalist business can go towards experiences such as travel and learning.</p>
<p>In my mind experiences are what life is worth living for, not possessions.</p>
<p>Last year, when I quit my job with $3,000 in the bank and moved to Portland, Or. I had 97 things. By the time I wandered back to the East coast, I had 75 things. When Alix and moved to San Francisco bay, I reduced my possessions to 50 things and a few weeks after I confessed that I really needed to live with <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/how-to-live-with-50-things/">more stuff than just 50 things</a>.</p>
<p>Well, I just did one of my occasional stuff-counts, and it looks like I was right but not by much.</p>
<p>Even after saying that I was going to scale up the stuff, I only have 57 things.</p>
<p><strong>Disclaimer that every minimalist blogger does about their stuff:</strong></p>
<p>Now, obviously Alix and I have a bunch of shared items such kitchen and bedroom stuff. We got two used stools for our kitchen, we found an inexpensive couch and chair. We have a bed. I also hang my jackets on hangers. So before you leave a comment asking if I sleep on a bed, the answer is yes, I do.</p>
<p>Our kitchen stuff is just the essentials, but we recently purchased a blender/food processor which is decidedly un-minimalist, but allows us to have one of the best simple breakfasts known to man: freshly made fruit smoothies. My energy levels have skyrocketed and I&#8217;ve continued to lose weight because of this investment.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m counting my underwear and socks together because every other minimalist blogger does too. We&#8217;re allowed to cheat on undergarments because we always have. <img src="/assets/posts/why-i-live-with-57-things-and-what-they-are-img2.gif" alt=":)" class="wp-smiley"/> </p>
<p>I also have a few books that I&#8217;m reading right now. I usually pass my books on to people who really need them. For instance, I just read Ramit Sethi&#8217;s <em><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B003I1WY0M?ie=UTF8&amp;tag=farbeythestam-20&amp;linkCode=as2&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=390957&amp;creativeASIN=B003I1WY0M">I Will Teach You to be Rich</a></em>, which I&#8217;m giving to my brother because he really needs some personal finance advice at the moment.</p>
<p><strong>Big purchases since moving to Cali.</strong></p>
<p>I&#8217;ve made two big purchases since moving to Cali. The first was a new bike, a <a href="http://surlybikes.com/bikes/steamroller_complete/">Surly Steamroller fixie</a>. I&#8217;ve always wanted one, so I decided now was the time. The bike market here is outrageous, so I would have spent almost as much on a good used bike as this simple fixed gear bike, so I opted to order it from online.</p>
<p>I also purchased the new iPhone 4, because my old iPhone screen broke when I was in Brooklyn. I use the iPhone as a way to keep in touch with readers over Twitter and to write 85% of my blog posts. I&#8217;ve also started using Internet tethering on the iPhone which gives me Internet anywhere that I go and eliminates the need for Internet in our house &#8211;though there are bandwidth restrictions, which might be problematic if you watch a lot of TV online. We&#8217;ve gone over a once so far but it still seems to be cheaper than a dedicated line.</p>
<p><strong>In the outbox:</strong></p>
<p>A good minimalist always has an outbox, and I&#8217;ve put one thing in mine that I think are important to note here.</p>
<p>I decided recently that <strong>I should give away my Canon SLR camera</strong>. I haven&#8217;t used it more than once since moving to San Francisco, and only a dozen times in the last year. It definitely isn&#8217;t fitting into my &#8216;one-month-rule&#8217;, and so needs to go. This is hard for me, because I was a photographer for a long time. As some of you remember, I closed down my photography business for good around six months ago in order to focus exclusively on writing.</p>
<p>To be honest, I&#8217;ve made 2000% more as a writer than I ever did as a photographer, so that was a good move from a business perspective. It&#8217;s still hard, because the camera cost so much in the first place and the resell value is incredibly low now that consumer cameras are everywhere.</p>
<p>All of the photos you see on this blog were taken on my iPhone (including the one above) &#8212; <a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/">you can see all of my photos here</a>. It&#8217;s honestly way better for casual captures than my Digital Rebel ever was.</p>
<p>I&#8217;ve discarded a significant amount of clothing because of wear and tear over the last few months as well, so my clothes have actually become more streamlined. For instance, I used to have two hoodies, now I only have one new one. However, I&#8217;ve purchased more underwear, which has helped with not having to do laundry so much. I also have more pairs of jeans and more shoes than I used to.</p>

<p>Obviously you don&#8217;t need much to have a great life. I certainly don&#8217;t have much of anything.</p>
<p>The point is to focus your possessions around what really matters to you, so this number will be bigger or smaller depending on your specific interests.</p>
<p>I live a location independent life working on the Internet, my main interests are writing and practicing yoga. This allows me to really only need two things for my interests: a yoga mat and a computer. Your life might be different, and it&#8217;s okay to have more.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m flexible with my things though, so I may buy more things. I may get rid of them. Who knows, I&#8217;m not really stressing about a specific number. Once I start traveling, I imagine I&#8217;m going to travel with less than 57 things, because I have a home-base here in Oakland now.</p>
<p>Why carry so many things when I can travel with 25 or 30 things?</p>
<p>The idea is that we need to curb our consumerism in order to focus on the important. This is why I live with less, because I&#8217;ve decided to stop consuming and start living.</p>
