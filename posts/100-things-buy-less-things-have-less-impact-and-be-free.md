<p><strong>The history and reason behind of 100 things.</strong></p>
<p>100 Things is a challenge originally issued by <a href="http://www.guynameddave.com/100-thing-challenge.html">David Michael Bruno</a>. The idea behind it is that human beings only need 100 things to survive, but most of us have way more than that. By restricting ourselves to 100 things, we&#8217;re creating less waste, we&#8217;re more flexible in how and where we live. It&#8217;s a win-win situation! One of the more famous bloggers who has <a href="http://mnmlist.com/100-things/">100 things is Leo Babauta</a>.</p>
<p>I mentioned in my <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?p=323">100 Minimalist Ideas</a> article that I had 79 things. I was wrong, I have 97 things!</p>
<p></p>
<p>You&#8217;ll notice that some of these things are in New York, in a box that my friends are keeping at my old apartment at the School House in Brooklyn &#8212; I&#8217;ll be fetching these at some point. I also have some warm winter clothing in Chicago, which I sent to my mom when I left New York. I&#8217;ve counted all of these in my 100 things, even though they&#8217;re not with me right now.</p>
<p>I did NOT group my underwear or socks into one thing. I believe it&#8217;s fine to cheat on underwear and socks, but I didn&#8217;t need to so I listed them all. You&#8217;ll also notice that I don&#8217;t have any kitchen stuff (except the coffee grinder in New York,) this is because I&#8217;ve only lived in shared houses where kitchen stuff was already present. I&#8217;m probably never going to live by myself, so these things will always be shared. I also didn&#8217;t count food that I have in the fridge, because I&#8217;m going to eat that. I also didn&#8217;t count absurd things like cords and power plugs, assume these go with the computer, camera, hard drive, etc.</p>
<p>You&#8217;ll also notice that I have no books or CDs. I read my books in the library, or at the bookstore. When I do buy books, I donate them or sell them after I finish reading them &#8212; they&#8217;re in my head, I don&#8217;t need them anymore. Books are freakin heavy, why don&#8217;t we put them back into the system after we&#8217;re done with them? I mp3ed all of my CDs years ago, and donated them to the universe. I get all of my music online now.</p>
