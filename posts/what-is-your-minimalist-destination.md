<p><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-738" title="peacetree" src="/assets/posts/what-is-your-minimalist-destination-img1.jpg" alt="peacetree" width="250" height="334"/>Being minimalist is having the flexibility to do what you want, when you wish to do it.</p>
<p><strong>Think about it, if you wanted to, could you do these things:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Can you fly to Peru next Friday?</li>
<li>Can you start your own company in a month?</li>
<li>Can you relocate to Vancouver next week?</li>
<li>If you lost your job, would it be devastating?</li>
</ul>
<p>I don&#8217;t believe you should just be minimalist for the sake of being minimalist. The philosophy has to have another reason, and it&#8217;s important to write that down.</p>
<p>Think of something impossible, an objective that you&#8217;ve always wanted to achieve, but that everyone told you was impractical. Make that your goal for next year.</p>
<p>Write that goal down.</p>
<p>When I quit my job and flew to Portland Oregon in August, it was easy, because I could carry all of my stuff. I lived a sustainable life, so surviving on $3,000 for three months wasn&#8217;t a problem while I worked my freelance contacts online.</p>
<p>Many people are trapped in their own lives by their stuff. But the reality is, we don&#8217;t need any of it anymore.</p>
<p>Despite what they might tell you on television news, we live in an age of abundance.</p>
<p>You can have everything you ever wanted, it&#8217;s down at the corner store. It&#8217;s important to recognize that you can have everything that you want, but if you limit yourself to the essentials you will open a world of possibilities for yourself. You can live anywhere, you can work in anywhere.</p>
<p>It doesn&#8217;t matter who you are, the possibilities are open if you get rid of the physical, mental, and emotional stuff.</p>
<p>You can be free. You can do the impossible.</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s my &#8216;impossible&#8217; goal for next year:</p>
<p>I want make Far Beyond The Stars my primary source of income. A handful of people have told me this is impossible, but I don&#8217;t believe that is true now. <a href="http://www.alistbloggingbootcamps.com/introduction-2/">Leo Babauta has done this</a>, <a href="http://chrisguillebeau.com/3x5/overnight-success/">Chris Guillebeau has done this</a>. Many others make $60,000-$100,000 a year off their blogs.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m aiming for a more modest $30,000. That&#8217;s a little more than I need to survive, with those extra dollars I&#8217;ll buy my girlfriend a birthday present.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m going to keep writing posts that help people. I want to continue to teach people how to simplify their lives and be happier.</p>
<p>How can you help me do this? Share my stories with a few friends. If you enjoy an article, there is a &#8216;share&#8217; option at the bottom of the post. Post my story to Twitter, Facebook, or email it to a couple of folks. This is so easy, it takes 10 seconds, and it&#8217;s the only way that my articles reach new people.</p>
<p>Only share my post if you enjoyed it, only do this if you think they will enjoy reading it too.</p>

<p>I created a <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?page_id=729">support page</a>, which has a donate button on it. If you ever feel like my words are bringing value to your life, if they help in any way, consider giving a small amount.</p>
<p>If only a handful of people give a small amount this year, my goal is no longer an impossibility.</p>
<p>&#8211;</p>
<p>What impossible goals will you set for yourself next year? How will you achieve them?</p>
