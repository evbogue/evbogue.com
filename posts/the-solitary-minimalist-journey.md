<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4274364475/"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/the-solitary-minimalist-journey-img1.jpg" alt="" width="263" height="350"/></a>Over the last couple of months I&#8217;ve received a number of comments and tweets from people who want to share their minimalist ambitions with their spouse or their entire family.</p>
<p>Some were so enthusiastic, they wanted to make all of their friends minimalists too!</p>
<p>This is great, I love that people are so enthusiastic.</p>
<p>However, the idea of making other people minimalist is a difficult question.</p>
<p>Many people relate to objects differently. I&#8217;ve met people who live with nothing, and are perfectly content. I&#8217;ve met people who would kill themselves if they lost everything &#8212; even if this would inevitably lead to them being freer.</p>
<p>Many people still subscribe to the (antiquated, in my opinion) mentality that we are what we own. It&#8217;s can be hard to change these minds.</p>
<p>So, here&#8217;s my suggestion:</p>
<p><strong>Don&#8217;t worry about the other people. You can&#8217;t force them to be minimalists.</strong></p>
<p>Instead, set a good example with yourself.</p>
<p>Adopt a <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?p=702">30 day rule</a> for your stuff, get rid of all your stuff and start to <a href="http://www.farbeyondthestars.com/?p=429">live with 100 things</a>.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t bother your boyfriend about his pile of dusty CDs. Focus all of that effort you&#8217;re directing at another person into freeing your own life.</p>
<p><strong>Lead by example. </strong></p>
<p>Show the people who you wish were less attached to their stuff how free you&#8217;ve made your own life.</p>
<p>Consider starting a blog documenting your progress becoming a minimalist. This can only help communicate your intentions. Join a <a href="http://www.yousimplified.net/2010/01/12/15-simplicityminimalist-bloggers-to-watch-in-2010/">growing community of amazing bloggers</a> writing about being minimalist.</p>
<p>With any luck your partner, your kids, and your friends will start to embrace the idea of lessening their connection to their material possessions, and begin to adopt a freer lifestyle.</p>
