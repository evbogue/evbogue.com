<p>Yesterday I took Yoga from a teacher who  was simply reading the manual.</p>
<p>She said all of the stuff you&#8217;re supposed to say in a yoga class, but there was no  feeling, no connection, no emotion. She was just doing what she was told.</p>
<p>There&#8217;s  a huge difference between intellectualizing a process and creating an  experience worth living.  I suppose  that&#8217;s what I try to do with this blog. I want to create an experience  that makes you think about the implications of your actions.</p>
<p>I could just read the <a href="http://www.viperchill.com/">blog manual</a> and  create a successful blog. Something would be missing though. The heart,  the soul, the quality of the work.</p>
<p>Yes,  that means that occasionally I tell you things that challenge your  perception of reality. Sometimes I tell you things that a generation ago  would have been so completely untrue that they&#8217;re accepted as false by  most people still.</p>
<p>Yes, you can work  from anywhere.</p>
<p>Yes, you do not need many things.</p>
<p>Yes, advertising has  enslaved you.</p>
<p>That doesn&#8217;t mean the things on fringes of reality are false. Conventional wisdom is often very wrong, because it&#8217;s conventional.</p>
<p>Life is a balance between reading the manual &#8212; the public  record, the modern mythos of what is acceptable and what is not &#8212; and pushing the boundaries of what the rest of everyone thinks is acceptable.</p>
<p>I understand that it&#8217;s scary to wakeup  from the American dream and realize there is another reality which might  just be better. You could aspire to  owning a McMansion and filling all the closets. You could find your freedom in French Fries.</p>
<div>
<p>Or, maybe you&#8217;d rather go vagabonding.  You&#8217;ll find the secret of life on the road, in the hills and valleys, in  the trees and the swamps. Silence in the newness of everything is  beautiful.</p>
<p>Or you could read the  established manual. Do what they told you to do in school. Get the house, the career  in a dying industry, the aspirations of generations past. That life  might be possible too, I admit that. But honestly, is it really what you  want? Especially if this is the blog you&#8217;re reading.</p>
<p>I think not.</p>
<p>This minimalist life  has no manual &#8212; though I&#8217;m did my best to write a <a href="http://www.artofbeingminimalist.com/">small guidebook</a>. <a href="https://www.e-junkie.com/ecom/gb.php?ii=329069&amp;c=ib&amp;aff=91858&amp;cl=10747">Leo&#8217;s simple guide</a> is brilliant too, but a guide can&#8217;t explain every nuance of every situation. You have to make your own decisions eventually, you have to pick a point where you decide to go where the map hasn&#8217;t been written.</p>
<p>There is no right way that&#8217;s been tread  before on this path. Yes, it&#8217;s terrifying to open yourself to infinite  possibilities. Yes, maybe something will go wrong. Yes, it&#8217;s you&#8217;ll make  mistakes and take the wrong road once in awhile.</p>
<p>But isn&#8217;t that better than taking the same road, day after  day, that millions of others have already tread?</p>
<p>I think it is.</p>
