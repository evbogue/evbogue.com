October 29, 2011

I'm sitting in Cafe Oro Verde, the only cafe in Puerto Vallarta where they roast their own beans. The owner Wen tells me that the coffee is grown 30 kilometers from the cafe in the jungle above Puerto Vallarta.

I'm writing a letter, from me to you, on [Evolving Your Digital Work](/posts/evolve). I take notes from that notebook (above) and riff on them in depth in [Scrivener](/posts/how-i-write). Then? I'll push the publish button, mañana.

I'm writing about:

- How do I discover a sense of certainty in the uncertainty of movement through the world?
- How I got from San Francisco to Puerto Vallarta
- What happened to the location independent dream?
- Looking back on Seattle, and looking forward into the future.
- And how virtual worlds support my life in the physical.

It's cool stuff, and there's no archive on Evolving Your Digital Work. I'll be sending the Letter out this afternoon (Saturday, October 29th) at some point in the afternoon. If you sign up before 2pm CST, you'll definitely receive it.

From Puerto Vallarta with coffee,
Ev Bogue
