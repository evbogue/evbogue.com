<p><a href="/assets/posts/do-not-listen-to-anyone-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft size-full wp-image-792" title="dontlisten" src="/assets/posts/do-not-listen-to-anyone-img1.jpg" alt="" width="250" height="333"/></a>Many people I meet have a story about someone they know who failed.</p>
<p>People love to tell me these stories.</p>
<p>Maybe you&#8217;ve heard them too?</p>
<p>There&#8217;s the one about the girl who moved across the country and couldn&#8217;t find a job and &#8216;now it&#8217;s so hard for her&#8217;.</p>
<p>Or that one boy who decided he was going to be a musician and went to New York and he works retail and &#8216;it&#8217;s so hard for him&#8217;.</p>
<p>Everyone knows someone like this. They keep this person&#8217;s story ready to go, and take aim at your dreams with him or her as evidence. &#8216;Look at this person, they couldn&#8217;t do it, you can&#8217;t either&#8217;.</p>
<p>Then there&#8217;s the story about some mythical creature that &#8216;puts in the long hours for their company, and now they&#8217;re set for life.&#8217;</p>
<p>No one seems to know anyone like this, and yet they&#8217;re all so sure this is a reality.</p>
<p>Where are these &#8216;set for life&#8217; people? I want to meet one please. Have one of them send me a post card.</p>
<p>Everyone I know who is doing well right now has their own established remarkable reputation.</p>
<p>The most common variety of person I encounter is settled for life. They gave up long ago, and now they drag out that story every time I tell them I&#8217;m moving again to pre-prove that I can&#8217;t do it &#8230;again.</p>
<p>Here&#8217;s my view of reality: you can do anything you want to, as long as you&#8217;re willing to sacrifice your ability to undermine yourself.</p>
<p>There are no failures, only the people who choose to use other brave people as defense against their own settled mediocrity.</p>
<p>Don&#8217;t listen to anyone&#8217;s fail stories. They just keep them in their back pocket to defend their decision to be scared, to stay put, to not change.</p>
<p>You can move anywhere and do anything if you&#8217;re willing to trade secure normality for knowing that you&#8217;re doing something awesome.</p>
<p>Where are you going next year?</p>
