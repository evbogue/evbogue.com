<p><a href="http://www.flickr.com/photos/bogue/4326527186/"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/how-anticipation-is-the-least-minimalist-emotion-img1.jpg" alt="" width="263" height="350"/></a>Around two million years ago, we humans evolved the absolutely stunning ability to see things that were going to happen in our heads.</p>
<p>Two million years might seem like a long time, but as far as evolution goes we&#8217;ve just developed this miraculous ability. We&#8217;re still trying to grasp how to use our pre-frontal cortex properly.</p>
<p>This ability to project experiences upon ourselves can be quite useful. It allows me to try out blog post topics in my head before I write them. If they bomb in my head, there is a good chance you won&#8217;t want to read them either. This saves me the trouble of writing (too many) bad blog posts.</p>
<p>I like to call this our futuresense.</p>
<p>No other animal has this ability to anticipate the future quite like humans do. It&#8217;s hard to compare it with any other ability. I trust you can do this, and you know what I&#8217;m talking about.</p>
<p>This ability to futuresense is cool, but the problem is that it doesn&#8217;t always work right. In fact, a good deal of the time our futuresense is completely off.</p>
<p><strong>We don&#8217;t know what will make us happy.</strong></p>
<p>I gaurantee at some point in your life you&#8217;ve sat awake at night and anticipated a date that was going to change your life &#8212; and then it was a boring date. Or you couldn&#8217;t wait to get your hands on a new gizmo-gadget, but this didn&#8217;t end up solving all of your problems.</p>
<p>If fact, imagine you have this choice: Tomorrow you&#8217;re either going to win the lottery, or you&#8217;ll be hit by a bus and be paralyzed for the rest of your life.</p>
<p>Which one will make you happier? Easy choice, I know. You want to win the lottery.</p>
<p>Well, you guessed wrong.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.ted.com/talks/lang/eng/dan_gilbert_asks_why_are_we_happy.html">Studies have been done on the happiness</a> of these two outcomes. Statistically people who experience these two outcomes end up with the same level of happiness, whether or not they were hit by a bus or won the lottery. Shocking!</p>
<p>What this tells me is that we&#8217;re all too focused on a future happiness, and ignoring the fact that we&#8217;re going to be the same amount of happy in the future as we are now.</p>
<p>Why don&#8217;t we appreciate what we have now? Stop futuresensing, start enjoying this moment.</p>
<p><strong>We can&#8217;t futuresense most outcomes.</strong></p>
<p>Then there is the fact that of all the possible outcomes that you&#8217;ve been futuresensing, the chances of anything close to what you futuresense coming true are slim.</p>
<p>Many of us sit for endless hours pondering outcomes. &#8216;Well if I do this, x will happen and it&#8217;ll be a disaster.&#8217;</p>
<p>How many times has your futuresense told you that everything was going to be a big mess, and it wasn&#8217;t? Probably most of the time. The world isn&#8217;t really that scary a place, but your futuresense still thinks there are sabertooth tigers.</p>
<p><strong>You can&#8217;t anticipate the future.</strong></p>
<p>So we spend hours upon hours &#8216;making plans&#8217; before we do anything. If what I&#8217;m telling you about the inaccuracies of our futuresense is true, then making plans is about the most absurd thing you can do.</p>
<p>By making a plan, you&#8217;re just sitting around contemplating a future that isn&#8217;t going to happen. Your end product is zero. This doesn&#8217;t mean you can&#8217;t have objectives, priorities, goals.</p>
<p>I&#8217;m just saying that playing out the final act in your head before you start working is a waste of time, and yet we spend so much time doing it.</p>
<p><strong>Nothing actually happens until you take an action and do something.</strong></p>
<p>You can&#8217;t futuresense outcomes at all, you can only waste your time. It&#8217;s the least minimalist emotion.</p>
<p>You have the power to decide to opt out of using your pre-frontal cortex before you do every little thing. This is not easy, but it helps once you recognize what you&#8217;re doing. You&#8217;re working yourself up into a big old panic attack over 75 futures that won&#8217;t happen.</p>
<p>Turn it off, start living your life.</p>
