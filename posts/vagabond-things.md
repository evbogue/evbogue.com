*October 17, 2011*

I'm getting on train headed south from Seattle today. I'm not sure where I'm going, but I'll let you know when I get there. I'll be writing more about what's up in my world on Evolving Your Digital Work.

One of the most popular posts I wrote here on Evbogue.com last month was when I wrote about my [personal Things](/posts/personal-things). (Actually, the most popular posts I've ever written ever over the past two years of living out of a backpack have been about what I pack. I shouldn't be so surprised.)

I untethered from a whole bunch of stuff since the last post on my Things, and made some significant upgrades to my wardrobe.

**I've been living out of a bag for over two years now.** Over time, I've streamlined what I need significantly. I used to pack three times as much as this when I didn't know where I was going.

I don't have a secret storage locker in the world somewhere with junk in it. These are all of my Things.

Here is what I pack when I don't know where I'm going:

Nau Jacket
Outlier Wool Pullover
Grey T-shirt
Vibrams (these are women's Vibrams size 42. The men's are too wide for me.)
Two tanks
Two pairs of yoga pants
Wallet with cash-money $$
Grey soft T-shirt
Wool and non-wool toe socks
Leg warmers
Nau Jacket
Outlier Climbing pants
Outlier ride to work pants
Ibex woolies 3 pairs (I can wear these for days without them smelling, amazing.)
Outlier wool t-shirt
Grey long sleeve t-shirt
iPhone
Skullcandy earbuds
Macbook Air 11inch
Incase Laptop sleeve
Notebooks from Paper Hammer
Think and Grow Rich (more of an invocation than reading material now.)
Pocket knife for eating veggies
Hard drive
Little moleskine notebooks for to-dos, ideas
Passport and other docs
Pens
So I know which way reality is
Assorted necessary things for human existence
Jade Yoga travel mat
Small backpack

And everything packs up into my backpacking bag. Ta-da!
