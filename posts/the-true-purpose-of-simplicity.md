<p><a href="/assets/posts/the-true-purpose-of-simplicity-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/the-true-purpose-of-simplicity-img1.jpg" alt="" width="258" height="350"/></a>We sometimes forget why we&#8217;re here, we aren&#8217;t looking where we&#8217;re going, or even where we&#8217;ve been.</p>
<p>We get all caught up in an idea about what we should be doing, and forget about what we really want to do.</p>
<p><strong>I think what we want to do is to be free.</strong></p>
<p>Instead we&#8217;re told by society that we&#8217;re supposed to buy a new car. We&#8217;re supposed to get our hair done a specific way. We&#8217;re supposed to go to college. We&#8217;re supposed to work all day and still somehow we&#8217;re in outrageous debt, and we wonder why.</p>
<p><strong>There&#8217;s a weird misunderstanding about what simplicity actually is. </strong></p>
<p>I get a lot of emails from people saying that they would never want to live this life.</p>
<p>For example, recently someone told me that the only reason they&#8217;d ever stop driving is if there was a mass extinction of the human race. I asked her why she was reading my blog if she was so opposed to everything it stands for.</p>
<p>The problem is, that I haven&#8217;t really defined what this blog is about, so it&#8217;s understandable that some people would be confused.</p>
<p><strong>Far Beyond The Stars is about one very specific thing:</strong></p>
<p>1. How to achieve freedom.</p>
<p>It&#8217;s not about skipping your coffee in the morning, it&#8217;s not about getting out of debt, it&#8217;s not about growing your food in your backyard, it&#8217;s not about checking yourself into the monastery on the corner and meditating 17 hours a day.</p>
<p><strong>The origins of my simplification, and how it helps you.</strong></p>
<p>I never intended to follow the set career path that society laid out for us. I didn&#8217;t go to high school, instead I took ballet and modern dance classes every day. I went to college to become an artist, and then hopefully at the end I secretly hoped that I would be inducted into the <a href="http://thelxd.com/">Legion of Extraordinary Dancers</a>.</p>
<p>But it didn&#8217;t happen.</p>
<p>Instead I watched in horror as society <strong>crushed the dreams</strong> of every single artist friend I had. One by one every single one of them settled in some way for an outcome that wasn&#8217;t what they intended.</p>
<p><strong>Slowly, one by one we didn&#8217;t make the audition for the dream that we&#8217;d always had. </strong></p>
<p>Instead, we went out and got jobs at restaurants, we stayed in the basement of the university library, we did a good interview at a corporate job and got fat and lazy sitting at a desk all day.</p>
<p>Maybe in our spare time we kept working on our art, secretly hoping that we&#8217;d get a record deal or a publisher would pull us up by our bootstraps.</p>
<p>Seldom does Deux Ex Machina happen in real life though &#8212; the only person who can save you is you.</p>
<p>I believe this is actually everyone&#8217;s story. Some of us made it farther along the road than others, like we actually got into dance school or we had one show at CBGB&#8217;s on the Bowery with a packed house before it closed.</p>
<p>Eventually I gave up and settled for a job, because everyone else did.</p>
<p>Four years later I woke up and realized that I was missing the point, that somewhere along the way we all did, and this is why we failed.</p>
<p><strong>The reality that was broken.</strong></p>
<p>So every morning I woke up and took the subway into work. I sat at a desk and made other people&#8217;s stories look nice (meanwhile being told every time I pitched an idea that I&#8217;d never be a writer- HA, now who&#8217;s the more successful writer?) It was fun, we thought we were doing good work. I was paid just enough to survive, but it was never enough. My student loans just sat there accumulating interest.</p>
<p>But slowly on the fringes of my social radar, I noticed as one by one <strong>people started to drop off the radar</strong>. They said &#8216;fuck you&#8217; to the corporations and started wandering the streets of America searching for the answer &#8212; what the tiny little voice in their back of their heads said.</p>
<p><strong>&#8220;There must be a better way.&#8221;</strong></p>
<p>What these people did, and what I did, was to radically refine our meaning of success.</p>
<p>We start to realize that the success that we thought we needed was implanted in our heads by the advertisers.</p>
<ul>
<li>Coca Cola wanted us to think success was sitting at the movies chugging cokes watching Tom Cruise dodge explosions.</li>
<li>American Airlines wanted us to think of success as once a year taking an expensive flight to the caribbean.</li>
<li>Nikon and Canon want you to believe that you&#8217;ll be a famous photographer if you just buy one more camera lens.</li>
<li>The Bush administration wanted us to think success was not getting blown to bits by terrorists (which statistically is much lower than the fact that <strong>you have a 1 in 100 chance of being crushed by a car</strong> or flying through your Subaru&#8217;s windshield) while getting our permission to bomb the crap out of a foreign country in order to keep oil prices low.</li>
<li>American Idol wanted us to think success was texting 1 to their magic number while we sat in our chairs and munched on Lean Cuisine.</li>
</ul>
<p>You get the idea.</p>
<p>Meanwhile I saw one by one my friends wake up and realize it was all a big fake magic reality that we&#8217;d have if we just bought one more Budweiser in the Meatpacking District.</p>
<p>We were in The Matrix, and the only freedom was truly to opt out.</p>
<p>So I did, I destroyed all of my stuff, quit my job, and got off the grid permanently.</p>
<p><strong>Far Beyond The Stars is about waking you up.</strong></p>
<p>It&#8217;s about telling you that your reality is broken, and there is another option.</p>
<p>You didn&#8217;t make the choices you made because you wanted to, you did it because The Man Behind The Television told you (because he wants your money.)</p>
<p><strong>The Internet gave us the tools to create this revolution</strong> in the way that we think. We no longer live in the illusion that buying one more video game will make us happier. We no longer believe that a fancy handbag will make us find love.</p>
<p>We no longer believe that success = Donald Trump.</p>
<p>Instead we&#8217;re slowing redefining freedom as a reality you can have right now, if you just stop consuming.</p>
<p>Destroy all of that crap the television told you to buy and never go to the mall again.</p>
<p>Because buying more isn&#8217;t the answer. Freedom is.</p>
<p>And that my friends, is what Far Beyond The Stars is about.</p>
