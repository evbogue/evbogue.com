<p><strong><a href="/assets/posts/when-you-take-it-all-away-what-are-you-img1.jpg"><img class="alignleft" src="/assets/posts/when-you-take-it-all-away-what-are-you-img1.jpg" alt="" width="270" height="360"/></a>Here&#8217;s a simple meditation:</strong></p>
<p>Imagine or actually do this.</p>
<p>Take everything that you owned away from you. You can do this by going to a park for a day and sitting under a tree, go to the beach and lie on a towel, or perhaps by hopping on a plane with a backpack for a month or two.</p>
<p>Now take a look at what&#8217;s left. Contemplate the remainder, without the stuff. The essence of what you really are without all of the junk.</p>
<p>Whatever is left when you get rid of the stuff is you.</p>
<p><strong>When you take away all that you&#8217;ve bought, what have you accomplished?</strong></p>
<p>Write this down.<strong> </strong></p>
<p><strong></strong>Now write down what you want to accomplish.</p>
<p>Perhaps you still have a small creative empire working without you or within you. Good!</p>
<p>Or perhaps you&#8217;ve just been surrounding yourself with distractions in order to forget that nothing you&#8217;ve ever done means anything.</p>
<p>Perhaps you&#8217;re somewhere in-between those extremes.</p>
<p><strong>Everyone reaches a point of no-return with their stuff.</strong></p>
<p>We humans reach a tipping point, when we&#8217;ve accumulated so much meaninglessness in our lives that there&#8217;s no going back. The form that this meaninglessness comes in is different for anyone. For some it&#8217;s buying the entertainment center, for others it&#8217;s their first house, for some it&#8217;s drinking every night to make the pain go away.</p>
<p><strong>From that moment forward, every dream is just a dream &#8212; they can never become reality. </strong></p>
<p>The dreams become so hard to make reality when you have to pay someone to move the entertainment system.</p>
<p>So you sit around dreaming about visiting Bali for the rest of your life. But you never actually go &#8212; except maybe for an extended weekend.</p>
<p>How can you replace your junk with real, meaningful, challenges and experiences?</p>
