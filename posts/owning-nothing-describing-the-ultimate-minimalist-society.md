<p><a href="http://www.twitter.com/zen_habits">Leo Babauta</a> just blogged at Mnmlist.com about a <a href="http://mnmlist.com/on-owning-nothing/">hypothetical minimalist society</a> where no one owned anything, I think this is an outstanding idea.</p>
<p>These are ideas that I subscribe to already, but there are limitations to how far you can take them in a society that feeds on permanent ownership and throwing objects out when you&#8217;re done.</p>
<p>However, there are methods that we can put into play to make a system like Leo describes work.</p>
<p><strong>Let&#8217;s develop some of those ideas, in my real world situation.<br/>
</strong></p>
<p>Obviously these get harder to execute with the more belongings you accumulate, but I think that&#8217;s kind of the point. If you cut down on your personal possessions than you can start to live in this society that Leo describes.</p>
<p><strong>Car Libraries:</strong><br/>
Zip Car has this down. Next week when my girlfriend flies in from New York we&#8217;ll be renting a Honda Hybrid and driving up to Seattle. We never would have been able to do this without Zip Car</p>
<p><strong>Housing Libraries:</strong><br/>
This isn&#8217;t easy, but I&#8217;ve seen it done. I&#8217;m doing it now, by renting a room month-to-month in Portland. I&#8217;m going to move out at the end of November and head to Chicago. We had a kind of a housing library system set up at The School House in Brooklyn&#8211;we had ten rooms in the place&#8211;and we subleased when someone was traveling. It was kind of like a housing library system, the only thing is that socially it was a lot more complicated than renting a house.</p>
<p>We need push for social change and start to install systems where it&#8217;s okay to rent a house for a week, or two months. We&#8217;ll call it Zip-house. Someone should get on this idea, there&#8217;s a lot of money to be made.</p>
<p><strong>Bike libraries:</strong><br/>
I&#8217;m really surprised that Portland doesn&#8217;t have these. They have a tool library, they have public libraries, but no bike libraries? I think I remember someone talking about how they tried to implement one and failed. Try again Portland.</p>
<p><strong>How I&#8217;m renting a bike:</strong></p>
<p>When I moved to Portland on September 21st, my first goal was renting a room, my second goal was buying an inexpensive bike that was nice enough that I could resell again just before I skipped town again. The bike cost me $140. I&#8217;m trying to resell it for at least that much before I leave on November 18th. It&#8217;s kind of like a free bike rental if I accomplish this.</p>
<p><strong>Clothes rental:</strong></p>
<p>This is harder, because clothes wear out. I personally just stick with a weeks worth of clothing, with a little extra underwear and socks thrown in. This way I just wear them until the clothes wear out.</p>
<p>It would be really nice if I could check a winter coat out of a library though, because right now my winter coat is in a box in my mom&#8217;s basement in Chicago, which I had to time ahead of time, because I knew I&#8217;d be in Chicago around the holidays.</p>
<p>Let&#8217;s build this minimalist society that Leo dreams of.</p>
